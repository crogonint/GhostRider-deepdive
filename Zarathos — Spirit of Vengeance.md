# Zarathos — Unbound Spirit of Vengeance

![](images/Zarathos.png)


> [!abstract] Designer Note — Empyreal Damage
> Zarathos's hellfire carries the **[empyreal]** descriptor. See [Canon Deep Dive](Canon%20Deep%20Dive.md) for full rules. Short version: bypasses all fire resistance, fire immunity, holy resistance, and unholy resistance. Evil and good creatures are equally vulnerable. Only the soulless and the genuinely innocent are immune.

> [!abstract] Inheritance Chain
> Zarathos inherits all abilities of Ghost Rider (CR 19) and Johnny Blaze (CR 6).
> Chain weapon feats are not inherited — Zarathos uses claws and natural attacks.
> All special qualities, knowledge skills, and fear mechanics are inherited and enhanced.

> Low for CR 26 tier (typical demon lords Wis 29–34). Intentional. Zarathos is primordial destructive force, not wisdom or judgment. He is notably more vulnerable to Will-based effects than his CR suggests — a deliberate character trait reflecting his CE nature and ancient chaos. He was enslaved by Mephisto using a soulless creature as a proxy. This is that weakness expressed mechanically.

## Auras — Three Active at All Times

> [!warning] 1. Aura of Ancient Dread (Su) — 120 ft. radius
> **Always active. Cannot be suppressed. Soul-based and supernatural — not mind-affecting.**
> As Ghost Rider's Aura of Existential Dread, enhanced. DC **35** Will save.
>
> | Soul Awareness Tier | Who | Fail | Success |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | **Tier 0** | No soul | Not immune — see Primordial Wrongness | — |
> | **Tier 1** | Animal soul | Flee + Frightened 1 round | −2 all checks |
> | **Tier 2** | Mortal soul | Frightened 1d4 rds (evil: Panicked) | Shaken 1d4 rds |
> | **Tier 3** | Aware soul | Panicked 1d4 rds | Frightened 1d4 rds |
> | **Tier 4 — evil outsiders** | Demon lords, devils, evil demigods | Panicked 1d4 rds + Cowering 1 round | Frightened 1d4 rds |
> | **Tier 4 — good outsiders** | Empyreal Lords, archons, angels | Panicked 1d4 rds | Shaken 1d4 rds — divine backing steadies them |
> | **Tier 5** | Soul-thieves | Panicked 1d4 rds + DC 35 or Cowering 1d4 rds | Frightened 1d4 rds + Shaken 1 round minimum |
> | **Tier 6** | Divine | Frightened 1d6 rds | Shaken 1d6 rds |
>
> **Death-workers with divine sanction:** Treat as Tier 2 at worst — same as Ghost Rider's aura.
>
> **Primordial Wrongness:** Tier 0 creatures (constructs, mindless, oozes) are not immune to Zarathos. They have no soul to judge, but the universe recoils from him. Tier 0 creatures within 120 ft. suffer −2 to all attack rolls and skill checks. Not a fear effect. No save. It is simply wrongness bleeding into reality.
>
> **Spellcasting:** DC 35 concentration check — as Ghost Rider's aura entry.

> [!warning] 2. Hellfire Aura (Su) — 30 ft. radius, cannot be suppressed
> 8d6 empyreal damage each round (DC 35 Ref half). Bypasses fire resistance up to 30 points. Fire-immune creatures still take 4d6 empyreal — the soul component cannot be blocked by elemental immunity.

> [!warning] 3. Smoldering Presence (Su) — 60 ft. radius, passive
> All non-magical plant life within 60 ft. dies within 1 round. Water evaporates within 10 ft. Constructed fires and lights within 30 ft. gutter and die unless magically maintained. Air becomes acrid and sulphurous. Animals within 120 ft. flee at maximum speed and do not stop. No damage, no save.

## Special Abilities

> [!note] Penance Stare + Judgment Lock (Su)
> **Standard action · Range 30 ft. · Soul-based, not mind-affecting**
>
> At full power, Zarathos's stare is not the controlled weapon Ghost Rider wields — it is a devouring, soul-stripping event.
>
> **Establishing contact:** Opposed Intimidate check (move action, Intimidate +60). As Ghost Rider entry.
>
> **Judgment Lock:** As Ghost Rider entry — paralyzed, no save to break, external intervention only.
>
> **DC 40 Will** (rolled once, on initial contact):
> - **Fail:** Paralyzed + **5d4 Wisdom damage per round**. At Wis 0: soul-dead. Only *wish*, *miracle* (DC 40 CL check), or a divine artifact restores them.
> - **Success:** Stunned 1 round + **1 Wisdom damage** (flat) per round the lock is maintained.
>
> **Benchmark rounds:** Street mortal ~0.7 · Elite caster ~2 · Demon Lord ~9 · Empyreal Lord ~11 · Lesser Deity ~22 · Greater Deity ~27

> [!note] Soul Binding (Su)
> **Standard action · Ranged touch +30 · Range 60 ft.**
>
> Zarathos manifests hellfire bonds that bind the target's soul, not just its body. Unlike Ghost Rider's physical chain, these are pure hellfire manifestations — they carry no weapon and require no proficiency.
>
> **On a hit:** 6d8 empyreal damage + soul binding (DC 35 Fort negates binding, not damage).
>
> **Soul-bound target:**
> - **Paralyzed** — cannot move or act. The soul is anchored.
> - **Cannot use any magical movement** (teleport, plane shift, ethereal jaunt, dimension door, etc.)
> - **−10 penalty** to all concentration and caster level checks
> - **4d8 empyreal damage per round** automatically while bound
> - **Field of Suppression:** All magical enhancements on the target suppressed as per *greater dispel magic* (CL 26, automatic — no check) for duration
>
> **Breaking free:** DC 42 Strength check (full-round action) OR *miracle* / *wish*-level magic OR artifact intervention. Standard magic does not work. *This is not a grapple. No CMB/CMD is involved. The soul is pinned to reality itself.*

> [!note] Soul Rend (Su)
> **Triggered on bite hit · DC 37 Fort**
>
> On a failed save: 1d4 Cha drain + sickened. Each subsequent round: DC 35 Will or take 1d4 more Cha drain. Two consecutive Will saves end the ongoing effect. Creatures reduced to 0 Cha are soul-dead — *raise dead*, *resurrection*, etc. all fail. Only *wish*, *miracle* (DC 37 CL check), or a divine artifact can restore them.

> [!note] Soul Consumption (Ex/Su)
> **Swift action** — usable on a freshly slain creature (within 1 round of death) or any soul-dead victim.
>
> Each soul consumed: regain **50 hp** + **+2 profane bonus** to attacks and saves (stacks, max +10, duration 1 hour). Consumed souls cannot be restored by less than 9th-level magic without a DC 45 CL check.
>
> *The more souls he consumes, the more powerful he becomes. This is not merely a mechanical fact — it is a narrative threat.*

> [!note] Possession (Su)
> **Full-round action · Range 60 ft. · DC 37 Will negates**
>
> Functions as *magic jar* (CL 26). Possessed host is dormant unless it wins a DC 37 Will save at the start of each turn. Zarathos uses his own physical ability scores. Cannot be expelled except by exorcism/banishment (DC 43) or specific artifacts. Standard *protection from evil* and *magic circle* grant full immunity. Zarathos cannot re-possess Johnny Blaze without the Unbreakable Bond activating the merge.

> [!note] Brimstone Upheaval (Su)
> **3/day · Standard action · 60 ft. radius**
>
> Zarathos drives his will into the earth. Choose one effect:
>
> **Stalagmites:** Ground splits, spiked eruptions (6d8 piercing + 4d8 empyreal, DC 35 Ref half + avoid impalement). Entangled and cannot move 1d6 rounds on failed Ref; DC 35 Str (full-round) to break free.
>
> **Stone Trap:** Ground hardens around targets (DC 35 Ref or partially encased — entangled, cannot move 1d6 rounds; DC 35 Str full-round to break free).
>
> **Thunderstorm (full round to build, then):** Lightning bolts each round — +30 ranged touch, 10d8 electrical + 6d8 empyreal, up to 4 bolts per round targeting separate creatures, for 1d6 rounds.

> [!note] Cold Hellfire Torrent (Su)
> **3/day · Standard action · 30 ft. cone**
>
> A wave of cold empyreal fire. Deals no physical or heat damage. Inflicts **6d6 Wisdom damage** (DC 35 Fort half). Creatures reduced to 0 Wis are soul-shocked — consciousness expelled, drifting without anchor until Wis is restored. Zarathos's most feared area tool — it strips the minds of everyone in a cone without physically harming them.

> [!note] Scent Sin (Su)
> At unlimited range on the same plane, Zarathos detects evil creatures by the "scent" of their sins. He knows direction, distance (precise within 1 mile, general beyond), and severity. The genuinely innocent have no scent and are entirely undetectable by this ability — they register as absence.

> [!note] Soul Hunger (Ex)
> If Zarathos goes 24+ hours without consuming a soul: cumulative **−2 penalty** to all d20 rolls (max −10). Resets on consumption. At −10, he becomes increasingly reckless and prone to tactical errors. *GM note: This is a campaign tool for depleting Zarathos in a long siege without direct combat.*

> [!note] Soulless Bane (Ex)
> Creatures with no soul — truly soulless beings like Centurious (the Soulless Man) — render Zarathos's soul-based abilities completely useless. Penance Stare, Soul Rend, Soul Consumption, and Soul Binding all fail automatically against soulless targets. Additionally, Zarathos takes **−4 to attacks and saves** while within 30 ft. of a truly soulless creature. This is his primary weakness beyond Heaven-forged weapons and is the exploit Mephisto used to defeat him.

> [!note] Tongues (Su)
> **Constant**
>
> Over 21,000 years bonded to mortal hosts across every culture, Zarathos has accumulated comprehensive knowledge of mortal language. Functions as the *tongues* spell (permanent, CL 26). Speaks and understands every mortal language ever spoken, including dead languages. Communicates telepathically with any creature that has a language within 300 ft.

> [!note] Host Limiter (Ex)
> When possessing a host who is actively resisting (winning Will saves), Zarathos is treated as having **half his normal ability score bonuses** to attack rolls, saving throws, and special ability DCs. When the host is winning but not yet free, Zarathos surges — he spends actions trying to overwhelm the host's will (forcing additional DC 22–30 Will saves) rather than fighting external enemies. *The composite Ghost Rider represents Zarathos at approximately 50% of this block's power.*

> [!note] Burning Judgment Eyes (Su)
> Inherited and enhanced — as Ghost Rider entry. Constant, passive, all benefits.

> [!note] Hell-Tempered Leathers (Ex)
> Inherited — as Ghost Rider entry. In Zarathos form, the leathers manifest as blackened spectral armor that moves like cloth but strikes like iron.

> [!note] Undying (Ex/Su)
> Inherited and upgraded — Zarathos's Seed of Life: if slain, reconstitutes. Keyed to his divine commission. So long as God's will for vengeance persists, so does Zarathos. Timeline at GM discretion — narrative rather than mechanical.

## The Sword of Uri-El — Zarathos Resonance

> [!note] Wielding the Sword (Su)
> Zarathos can wield the Sword of Uri-El. The sword accepts him — not despite what he is, but because of it. He is the ancient fire. The sword is the ancient fire wearing a different shape. When it recognizes him, it becomes complete.
>
> **Proficiency:** Martial Weapon Proficiency (longsword) — listed in feats. Alternatively, the GM may rule proficiency inherent given the sword's willing acceptance of Zarathos as a bearer.
>
> **Attack (one-handed):** +46 (BAB +26, STR +19, WF +1) + insight +6 = +52 with sword active
> **Attack (two-handed):** +46 (same; STR 1.5× to damage = +28 damage)
> **Damage:** 1d8+5 (base) + 8d6 empyreal (Sacred Fire — Zarathos resonance) + STR bonus (+19 one-handed, +28 two-handed)
>
> **Zarathos Resonance — Full Stats:**

> |---|---|---|---|
> | Sacred Fire | +4d6 empyreal | +6d6 empyreal | **+8d6 empyreal** |

## GM Notes

> [!warning] Power Scaling
> At full unbound strength (no host, full soul reserves), Zarathos is competitive with Mephisto. Doctor Strange described an unrestricted Ghost Rider (Zarathos fully in control) as potentially capable of defeating World War Hulk — and was *terrified* by that prospect. Consider the **Advanced template** (+2 all stats, +2 CR = CR 28) for Zarathos at absolute peak with a full soul reserve.
>
> **Soul Reserves gauge:** Track souls consumed this session.
> | Souls | Bonus |
> |-------|-------|
> | 1–4 | +2 profane to attacks and saves |
> | 5–9 | +6 profane to attacks and saves |
> | 10+ | Apply Advanced template automatically |
>
> This makes soul consumption a real escalating threat in encounter design.

> [!tip] Encounter Design
> - **Opener:** Penance Stare on the most powerful/guilty target, Soul Binding on the most dangerous spellcaster simultaneously.
> - **The soulless counter:** A soulless creature, construct, or genuinely innocent person in the party is Zarathos's hard counter. He cannot stare them, cannot soul-rend them, and takes −4 near them. This is the Centurious exploit.
> - **Soul Hunger:** In a multi-session siege, denying Zarathos soul consumption is a viable attrition strategy. At −10 to all rolls, he becomes erratic and beatable.
> - **Brimstone Upheaval:** Stalagmites to zone melee; stone trap to immobilize spellcasters; thunderstorm for open terrain.
> - **Don't forget Tongues:** Zarathos speaks every mortal language and is a deeply cunning diplomat when it serves him. He may negotiate, offer deals, or monologue mid-combat.

## Lore & Background

Zarathos predates recorded human history. He is not merely old — he was ancient when the first Ghost Riders rode. He built a cult of soul-worshippers among a Native American tribe, was tricked and enslaved by Mephisto using the soulless Centurious as a proxy, and has been bonded to mortal hosts as punishment ever since. He is not a demon in the traditional sense — he is something older, something that predates the angelic/demonic split. This is why his hellfire is neither holy nor unholy but **empyreal**: the fire of divine judgment corrupted by 21 millennia of demonic bondage without losing its fundamental nature.

The line *"I never needed Blaze. I never needed him. I... I need him."* is the most revealing thing ever said about Zarathos. He has been in Blaze so long that the two are genuinely interdependent. Without a soul to anchor him, Zarathos begins to die. He is not just a demon who hates his host — he is a demon who cannot exist without one, and who has chosen, against every instinct, to accept that Johnny Blaze is the right host.
