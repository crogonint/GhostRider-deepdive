# Ghost Rider — Canon Reference

![](images/Ghost_Rider.png)


*This document covers the canonical basis for every significant mechanical decision in this build. It is not a rules document — the stat blocks handle that. This is the "why."*

---

## The Three Aspects

Ghost Rider exists as three distinct but continuous aspects of the same being.

**Johnny Blaze** is the anchor. He is not merely a vessel — he is the reason Zarathos functions at all. Without a soul to anchor him, Zarathos begins to die. Johnny's will is what keeps the composite being from becoming a mindless engine of destruction, and his humanity is what makes Ghost Rider capable of mercy, judgment, and restraint. He holds nine Guinness World Records. Doctor Strange has identified him as a potential Sorcerer Supreme. He has been to Hell three times.

**Ghost Rider** is the composite — Zarathos expressing himself through Johnny Blaze at roughly 50% of his full power, tempered by Blaze's will. This is the default form. In the comics this form has defeated all the Avengers in combat, fought Thor to repeated draws, and survived a Sorcerer Supreme's spells unharmed.

**Zarathos unbound** is what happens when Blaze loses control. Doctor Strange, one of the most powerful sorcerers in the Marvel universe, was *terrified* by this prospect — specifically stating that Zarathos in full control could defeat World War Hulk. This calibrates the CR 26 assignment. He is competitive with Mephisto at full strength with a full soul reserve.

## The Empyreal Descriptor

This is the most mechanically significant decision in the entire build, and it is entirely canon-derived.

Multiple Marvel sources describe Ghost Rider's hellfire as *"empyreal"* — from the Empyrean, the highest Heaven. It is not fire. It is not holy energy. It is not unholy energy. It is **divine judgment fire** — a third category that operates entirely outside the good/evil axis.

**The three canon proofs:**

1. Ghost Rider wielded the Sword of Uri-El — an archangel's sacred fire blade — without being harmed. The fires recognized each other as the same fundamental energy. A demon-bonded human should have been destroyed by contact with angelic sacred fire. He wasn't. They're the same thing.

2. Lucifer's demonic power could not bind Ghost Rider's soul to Hell. The Spirit of Vengeance is a heavenly entity wearing a demonic skin.

3. Ghost Rider's hellfire works equally against demons (who would resist holy fire) and against celestials (who would resist unholy fire). It affects both, ignoring both immunities.

**Build application:** The [empyreal] descriptor bypasses all fire resistance, all fire immunity, all holy resistance, and all unholy resistance. Demon lords immune to unholy fire, angels immune to hellfire — none of it matters. The only things fully immune are creatures with no soul (constructs, the mindless) and the genuinely innocent, who have nothing for the judgment fire to find.

## Hellfire

**Cold hellfire** is a distinct and separate thing from hot hellfire. The FASERIP source describes it as *"cold hellfire that affects the human spirit"* — it deals no physical or heat damage at all. This is the basis for the Cold Hellfire Touch (Wis damage) and Cold Hellfire Torrent abilities. The fire burns the soul without touching the body.

**The chain** responds to mental commands. It has been depicted summoned from Ghost Rider's chest, from his throat, and from his hands. Its upper length limits are described in Marvel Database as *"unknown."* Mechanically this is reach 20 ft. with Lunge; narratively the GM may treat it as longer in non-combat contexts.

**The chain binds the soul, not the body.** This is why it causes paralysis rather than the entangled condition, why it prevents all magical movement, and why standard magic cannot break it. The soul is anchored to the material plane.

**The Hellfire Shotguns** — Johnny carries two. Both appear when needed; he reaches behind his back and they are there, no sheath, no holster. The power was revealed to come from within Johnny himself — the weapons are conduits for Blaze's own suppressed hellfire potential, which is why they are dramatically supercharged in Ghost Rider's hands: the full power is finally available, rather than bleeding through a human intermediary. The Winchester Model 1887 (lever-action, sawed-off) form comes from the 2007 film. The Remington 11-87 (semi-automatic, sawed-off, pistol grip, pistol foregrip, brass-cased bandoliers) is the most likely model for the comic book depiction. The comics origin of the power itself is Ghost Rider Vol. 3 #14 (1991). Both weapons are inert in anyone else's hands.

## The Penance Stare

The Penance Stare forces a target to *"relive every pain they ever inflicted on any being — physical, emotional, spiritual — simultaneously and in totality."* Marvel.com specifies that targets can be rendered *"permanently catatonic."*

**The Judgment Lock** — the inability to break eye contact through willpower alone — is canonically correct. Once the stare is established, the target cannot resist it through any act of will. Only physical intervention (someone stepping between them) ends it. This is why it is a paralysis effect with no saving throw to break the lock, only a saving throw to resist the damage.

**Immunity question:** The stare does not work on the genuinely innocent — there is nothing to show them. It also does not work on the soulless. This is not a house rule; it is a direct implication of what the ability actually does. Ghost Rider cannot show a construct its sins because it has none.

**The Damnation Stare** is a separate ability Ghost Rider develops as King of Hell — it sends demons directly to Hell rather than catatonic them. It is not included in this build, which represents the classic Spirit of Vengeance rather than the King of Hell variant.

## Existential Dread

The fear Ghost Rider induces is not psychological. It operates at the soul level — it is the recognition of divine judgment by a being's own soul, regardless of whether the mind has the context to understand it.

**This is why the effect scales upward with awareness, not downward.** A mortal sinner feels dread and fear but may not understand why. A demon lord knows *exactly* what it means — and has been running from this moment since before recorded history. A lich chose to defy the natural order of the soul. Its phylactery is the sound of a locked door behind which its judgment waits. Ghost Rider is the one holding the key.

**Death-workers with divine sanction** — psychopomps, angels of death, heralds legitimately collecting souls — are not afraid of Ghost Rider. They recognize him as a peer authority. They are in the same business, working for the same ultimate employer, just in different departments.

**Soul-thieves** — those who imprison souls against natural law — receive the worst possible effect. Mephisto, liches with phylacteries, soul-consuming demons. These beings have the most to answer for and the clearest understanding of what answering for it means.

## The Bond

![](images/Heavens_Gate.png)


The bond between Johnny Blaze and Zarathos was not simply Mephisto's contract. It was later sealed by the archangel Zadkiel — and ultimately by an authority that overrides both Heaven and Hell. Mephisto cannot alter the terms of the bond. He cannot renegotiate it, add conditions, or directly break it. This is why he always works through proxies and deception rather than direct action against Johnny.

No cosmic entity can steal or permanently break the bond without direct divine override. Zarathos cannot be claimed by another Hell Lord.

**Blaze's Will** has no CR cap. Johnny can resist direct commands from any entity at any power level, including Mephisto. This is not magical resistance — it is the nature of the bond itself. The One Above All sealed it.

## Johnny Blaze — The Human

Johnny is not merely a weak form of Ghost Rider. He is a distinct character with distinct strengths.

- Nine Guinness World Records for stunt riding. *In practice: +4 to all Ride checks, reroll 1/scene.*
- Described as *"incredibly street smart"* across multiple sources. *In practice: +4 to Bluff, Perception, Sense Motive in urban and road environments.*
- Considered a Sorcerer Supreme candidate by Doctor Strange, based on his occult knowledge and the nature of his connection to Zarathos. *In practice: +4 to Knowledge (arcana, religion, planes) vs. demons, contracts, and hellfire.*
- Can produce a lighter-sized empyreal flame without transforming. It is orange-black, not orange-yellow. It cannot be suppressed.

The Hellfire Stigma — the faint dread that bleeds through even in human form — is not something Johnny controls. It is Zarathos showing through the skin.

## Zarathos — The Ancient

Zarathos is not a demon in the traditional sense. He predates the angelic/demonic divide. His hellfire is empyreal — divine judgment fire — that has been corrupted by 21 millennia of bondage without losing its fundamental nature. This is why he carries both the demon and empyreal type descriptors simultaneously, and why he has no Unholy Aura.

He built a cult of soul-worshippers among a Native American tribe in ancient history. He was enslaved by Mephisto through the Centurious exploit — using a soulless being as a proxy, which renders all of Zarathos's soul-based abilities useless. This remains his primary weakness.

**The line that defines him:** *"I never needed Blaze. I never needed him. I... I need him."* He has been in Blaze so long that the two are genuinely interdependent. Without a soul to anchor him, Zarathos begins to die. He chose, against every instinct, to accept that Johnny Blaze is the right host.

**Soul Hunger** is a campaign tool. Denying Zarathos soul consumption in a prolonged siege depletes him cumulatively (−2/day to all rolls, max −10). At −10 he becomes erratic and beatable. This is how you fight something you cannot simply kill.

**The Soulless Bane** — Centurious-type beings who are genuinely without a soul — render all soul-based abilities useless and impose a −4 penalty near them. This is the exploit that defeated him before. Any campaign that features Zarathos as an antagonist should use this.

## The Hellcycle

The Hellcycle was Johnny's ordinary motorcycle until the night Zarathos first possessed him. The transformation infused it with empyreal fire and anchored its existence to the bond.

The 120 ft./round tactical speed is a gameplay abstraction. The true speed is supernatural and effectively unlimited — canonically described as faster than Thor's thrown Mjolnir. For overland purposes: treat as 500 mph with no fuel, no maintenance, and no terrain limit. It can cross a continent in hours.

The jump arc depicted in comics involves launching off buildings and crossing entire city blocks. The previous stat block's 30×20 ft. cap was an arbitrary number with no canon basis. The arc is adjudicated by the GM based on launch height and angle.

It reforms after destruction. It cannot be permanently destroyed while Zarathos is bound.

## The Sword of Uri-El

The Sword of Uri-El (Uriel — the Angel of Presence, the Flame of God, guardian at the gate of Eden) is the canon proof that Ghost Rider's hellfire and divine sacred fire are the same fundamental energy. Johnny Blaze — a man who sold his soul to the devil, bonded to a demon older than civilization — lifted the sword and wielded it without being consumed.

The sword's judgment is not about past sins. It is about present purpose. That is why a man with Johnny's history was considered worthy.

The sword deals [empyreal] damage, the same descriptor as Ghost Rider's hellfire. In Ghost Rider's hands specifically, the two fires resonate — both damage dice and save DCs increase, because the same energy is amplifying itself.

A pure expression of evil cannot lift the sword. Mephisto has not held it.

## Abilities Not Statted

These are canonical but excluded from this build for specific reasons.

**Damnation Stare** — Sends demons directly to Hell rather than rendering them catatonic. This is the *King of Hell* variant of the Penance Stare. It is excluded here because this build represents the classic Spirit of Vengeance, not Johnny Blaze after his arc as Hell's ruler. Reserved for a King of Hell variant if the campaign goes there.

**Summoning other Ghost Riders** — Canon. Not statted. This is a narrative/campaign tool rather than a combat ability. At the GM's discretion.

**Time travel** — Two Ghost Riders traveled back in time in canon. Not statted. Plot device only.

**Space survival** — Vengeance (a kindred entity) survives open space. Not statted for this build — narrative only.

**Size alteration** — VS Battles confirms he grew to match Thor in one confrontation. Available as an optional GM addition (SLA: *enlarge person*, self only) if a campaign requires it.

**Anti-transmutation** — Skin-Bender could not affect his flesh. Could be added as Su immunity to transmutation while transformed. Excluded as a formal immunity here to keep the build clean, but the GM note stands.

## The Cosmological Position

Ghost Rider occupies a unique position in the Marvel cosmos — and by extension in this campaign setting. He is not a hero. He is not a villain. He is a divine instrument that happens to share a body with a human being.

He cannot be employed by evil. He cannot be turned against the innocent. He cannot be permanently destroyed while his commission stands. His fire burns the guilty and passes through the pure.

The One Above All — the supreme deity of the Marvel cosmos, the being who created and maintains the universe against the predation of the Great Old Ones and primordial entities — sealed the bond between Blaze and Zarathos. Mephisto cannot break it. No Hell Lord can claim Zarathos. The commission is active.

This is what the Penance Stare represents at its deepest level. It is not a weapon. It is a judgment. Ghost Rider does not decide whether the target is guilty — he simply opens the door and lets the target's own soul answer the question.
