# Hellfire Weapons

![](images/Hellfire_Chain.png)

> *"It is not a weapon. It is an argument. One that always wins."*

---

## The Principle

Any weapon a Ghost Rider touches can become a hellfire weapon. The empyreal fire flows through whatever the Host channels it through — a chain, a shotgun, a sword, a chair leg, their bare fists. The weapon is the conduit. The fire is the point.

Some weapons become permanently transformed — soaked in empyreal fire so deeply that they are artifacts in their own right. Others are temporary channels, burning for a moment and then going cold. The difference is the bond. A weapon the Host carries habitually, one tied to their identity and their story, absorbs the fire over time and becomes part of them. A weapon grabbed in desperation burns bright and brief.

Every hellfire weapon shares the [empyreal] descriptor: divine judgment fire that bypasses all fire resistance, all fire immunity, all holy resistance, and all unholy resistance. The only things immune are the soulless, the genuinely innocent, and true constructs with no soul to judge.

---

## The Hellfire Chain

The signature weapon of the Ghost Rider. It originates in the comics as an ordinary motorcycle chain that absorbed empyreal fire during Johnny Blaze's first transformation and became something else entirely — empyreal fire given physical coherence, shaped by will rather than physics.

The chain responds to mental commands. It has been depicted summoned from Ghost Rider's chest, his throat, and his hands. Marvel Database describes the upper bound of its length as *"unknown."* In one panel it wraps around a fleeing truck. In another it coils around a demon's throat from across a room. It weighs nothing until it strikes.

When Wolverine clawed Ghost Rider's face, the hellfire backfired through the chain and Logan's claws simultaneously. He didn't try that again.

Physical contact with the chain pins the target's soul to reality. Demons who rely on dimension door or plane shift to escape find themselves anchored, unable to flee. The chain doesn't restrain the body. It restrains what matters.

The chain belongs to the composite form — Ghost Rider, the fusion of Host and Spirit. Zarathos unbound does not carry it. He uses claws, teeth, and the Sword of Uri-El. The chain is the Rider's weapon. It exists because the Host exists.

## The Hellfire Shotguns

![](images/Hellfire_Shotgun.png)

Johnny Blaze carries two. Both appear when needed — he reaches behind his back and they are there. No sheath, no holster, no explanation.

**Comics (Ghost Rider vol. 3 #14, 1991):** Johnny was carrying an ordinary shotgun during a confrontation with Danny Ketch's Ghost Rider. When Ghost Rider grabbed the weapon, his hellfire passed through it and permanently transformed it. The power was later revealed to come from within Johnny himself — the guns are conduits for Blaze's own suppressed hellfire potential, not independent power sources. This is why they are dramatically supercharged in Ghost Rider form: the human intermediary is removed and the full fire flows through.

**Film (Ghost Rider, 2007):** A Winchester Model 1887 lever-action given to Johnny by Carter Slade before the final confrontation with Blackheart. Johnny thrust it into shadow and it transformed.

The Winchester 1887 (lever-action, sawed-off) fires twice per round. The Remington 11-87 (semi-automatic, sawed-off, pistol grip, brass-cased bandoliers) fires four times per round. Both are twelve-gauge. Both conjure their own empyreal rounds — they cannot be emptied. In anyone else's hands, they are inert: non-magical sawed-off shotguns, cold and ordinary.

**Motorcycle Conjuration:** A genuine canon ability. Johnny can conjure a mundane motorcycle by pointing the shotgun at any open surface (1/day). Ghost Rider can conjure a full Hellcycle by firing a barrel at the ground (3/day).

## Other Canon Weapons

**Bare fists / natural attacks:** Zarathos unbound doesn't carry weapons. His claws and bite channel empyreal fire directly. Any natural attack from a transformed Ghost Rider carries the fire.

**Improvised weapons:** In the comics, Ghost Rider has channeled hellfire through lampposts, car doors, park benches, and at least one villain's own weapon grabbed out of their hands. Anything the Host touches while transformed can become a temporary channel.

**Danny Ketch's chain:** Visually and functionally similar to Johnny's, but Danny's version is sometimes depicted as more controlled — precise strikes rather than wild sweeps. Same empyreal fire, different personality behind it.

**Robbie Reyes's chains:** Robbie manifests chains too, but his are powered by Eli Morrow's ghost rather than a Spirit of Vengeance. They may not carry the true [empyreal] descriptor — an open question in the lore.

## Our Adaptation

### The Hellfire Chain — Leveling Class

The chain manifests on transformation. Dismissed as free action. It is a **bonus weapon** — the Host's normal weapons still work. Proficiency is inherent to the Spirit's divine office.

| Level | Enhancement | Properties |
|-------|------------|------------|
| 1-6 | +1 | Empyreal (via Heaven's Brand) |
| 7-10 | +2 | Flaming (+1d6 empyreal) |
| 11-14 | +3 | Flaming burst |
| 15-18 | +4 | 20 ft. reach (swift action toggle) |
| 19-20 | +5 | Anarchic flaming burst |

**Soul Binding (Level 8, requires Medallion):** On confirmed crit, Fort save (DC 10 + 1/2 level + STR mod) or no magical movement for 1d4 rounds. Binds the soul, not the body.

### Heaven's Brand

While transformed, ALL of the Host's attacks deal bonus empyreal damage — the chain, their normal weapon, natural attacks, improvised weapons, everything.

| Level | Bonus Damage |
|-------|-------------|
| 1-9 | +1d4 empyreal |
| 10-14 | +2d4 empyreal |
| 15-19 | +3d4 empyreal |
| 20 | +4d4 empyreal |

All d4s. One die type. Doubled in Overburn (1d4→2d4, etc.). Deliberately conservative — the empyreal bypass does the heavy lifting against resistant targets.
