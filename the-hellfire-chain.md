# The Hellfire Chain

![](images/Hellfire_Chain.png)

> *"It is not a weapon. It is an argument. One that always wins."*

---

## Canon

The Hellfire Chain originates in the comics as an ordinary motorcycle chain — the kind looped over the handlebars of any biker's ride. The night Zarathos first possessed Johnny Blaze, the chain absorbed empyreal fire during the transformation and became something else entirely. It is no longer metal in any meaningful sense. It is empyreal fire given physical coherence, shaped by will rather than physics.

The chain responds to mental commands. It has been depicted summoned from Ghost Rider's chest, his throat, and his hands — it appears wherever it needs to be. Marvel Database describes the upper bound of its length as *"unknown."* In one panel it wraps around a fleeing truck. In another it coils around a demon's throat from across a room. It is as long as the moment requires, and it weighs nothing until it strikes.

When Wolverine clawed Ghost Rider's face, the hellfire backfired through the chain and Logan's claws simultaneously. He didn't try that again. The chain is not just a weapon — it is an extension of the Spirit's judgment. Physical contact with the chain pins the target's soul to reality itself. Demons who rely on dimension door or plane shift to escape justice find themselves anchored, unable to flee, while the fire does its work. The chain doesn't restrain the body. It restrains what matters.

In the FASERIP system (Marvel Super Heroes RPG), the chain is listed as a +4 equivalent weapon with soul-trapping properties. No maximum length entry. It responds to Ghost Rider's will as naturally as a hand responds to a brain.

The chain belongs to the composite form — Ghost Rider, the fusion of Host and Spirit. Zarathos unbound does not carry it. He uses claws, teeth, and the Sword of Uri-El. The chain is the Rider's weapon. It exists because the Host exists.

## PF1E Adaptation

The spiked chain is the closest published weapon chassis in Pathfinder. We built the Hellfire Chain as a supernatural weapon / minor artifact with scaling enhancement for the leveling class and fixed stats for the CR 19 NPC.

**Proficiency is inherent.** The chain is part of what Ghost Rider is — divine office, not learned skill. No Exotic Weapon Proficiency feat required.

**Empyreal descriptor.** All chain damage carries the [empyreal] tag, bypassing fire resistance/immunity, holy resistance/immunity, and unholy resistance/immunity. Only the soulless, the genuinely innocent, and true constructs are immune.

**Soul Binding.** The canonical soul-trapping is mechanized as a Fort save — on confirmed crits (leveling class, level 8+) or on any successful hit (CR 19 NPC). Failure means no magical movement: teleportation, dimension door, plane shift, ethereal jaunt — all locked. This is not a grapple. No CMB/CMD. The soul is pinned to reality.

**Variable length.** Combat reach is 10 ft. standard, extending to 20 ft. at level 15 (swift action toggle). In narrative contexts, GM may treat length as unlimited.

## Leveling Class Version

Manifests on transformation. Dismissed as free action. Bonus weapon — the Host's normal weapons still work.

| Level | Enhancement | Properties |
|-------|------------|------------|
| 1-6 | +1 | Empyreal (via Heaven's Brand) |
| 7-10 | +2 | Flaming (+1d6 empyreal) |
| 11-14 | +3 | Flaming burst |
| 15-18 | +4 | 20 ft. reach (swift action toggle) |
| 19-20 | +5 | Anarchic flaming burst |

**Soul Binding (Level 8, requires Medallion):** On confirmed crit, Fort save (DC 10 + 1/2 level + STR mod) or no magical movement for 1d4 rounds.
