# The Sword of Uri-El

![](images/Lahat.png)

*Major Artifact · Unique · No market price*

---

## Canon

Uri-El — Uriel — is the Angel of Presence, the Flame of God, the guardian set at the gate of Eden. In Hebrew tradition, the sword he carries is the *Lahat HaCherev HaMithappekhet* — "the flame of the ever-turning sword" (Genesis 3:24). In the greater Marvel cosmic hierarchy, Uriel is a genuine divine entity: he appeared before Mephisto himself to contest the soul of Noble Kale, nearly triggering a war between Heaven and Hell over the terms of one man's soul.

The sword predates recorded human history. It burns with sacred fire described in canon as *"imbued with the force of the divine"* and specifically called *"soul-searing"* by those who witnessed it. The flame is not hot, precisely. It is *accurate*. It finds what deserves to burn.

It appeared in Ghost Rider's story during the *Blaze* series (Vol 1 #11-12). Johnny Blaze, separated from Zarathos and at the lowest point of his human life, encountered the sword and was able to lift and wield it. This is canonically significant: a man who had sold his soul to the devil, who was bonded to a demon older than civilization, who had caused incalculable collateral damage — was considered *worthy* by Uri-El's blade. The sword's judgment is not about past sins. It is about present purpose.

This is also the canon proof that Ghost Rider's hellfire and the sword's sacred fire are the same fundamental energy. Johnny was not consumed when he held it. The fires recognized each other. In the language of the [empyreal] descriptor: they are both divine judgment fire. One is simply wearing a skull-shaped hat.

The sword does not speak in words. It communicates in the sensation of warmth or cold, in the ease or resistance of the hand that holds it, in the way the flame brightens or gutters. It has opinions about what it is being used for. It does not make noise when drawn. The world simply becomes quieter.

## PF1E Adaptation

The Sword of Uri-El is a major artifact — no market price, unique, cannot be replicated. It is not a weapon Ghost Rider carries routinely. It is a weapon that appears when *needed*, usually when a threat exists that Ghost Rider's standard arsenal is insufficient to permanently resolve. A demon lord with regeneration and divine immunity. An evil outsider that simply respawns in its home plane. An entity that cannot be killed — only *unmade*.

**Key adaptation decisions:**

**Empyreal descriptor, not holy.** The sword does not deal [holy] or [unholy] damage. Like Ghost Rider's hellfire, it deals [empyreal] — divine judgment fire that bypasses all elemental resistance, all holy resistance, and all unholy resistance. They are the same fire in different vessels.

**Willing Blade.** Semi-sentient (Ego 28). Full power for the genuinely good or neutral of intent. Diminished (+2, no fire) for the ambiguously evil. Cannot be lifted by the purely evil. Instantly incinerates chaotic/lawful evil outsiders on contact — no attack roll required.

**Resonance.** Ghost Rider's hellfire and the sword's fire recognize each other. Ghost Rider gets enhanced stats. Zarathos gets complete resonance — the sword becomes, in his hands, what it was always trying to be.

**Sacred Fire (Su):** +4d6 [empyreal] on all hits. Bypasses all fire/holy/unholy resistance. Deals no damage to the genuinely innocent. Illuminates 60 ft. bright (not suppressed by magical darkness).

**Soul-Searing Strike (Su):** Every confirmed hit, DC 30 Fort or 2d6 Wisdom damage (3d6 vs evil outsiders). At Wis 0: soul-shocked — consciousness expelled from body.

**Anathema Strike (Su):** On crit vs evil outsider, DC 35 Fort. Failure: **destroyed** — physically, spiritually, and conceptually. No resurrection. CR 20+ fiends: 20d6 empyreal + max HP permanently halved instead. Second failure within a year destroys even a demon lord.

## Wielder Restrictions

**Full wield:** Genuinely good or neutral intent. Not about the alignment box — about the absence of malice toward the innocent.

**Diminished wield:** Ambiguous evil (villain who protects family, anti-hero who feels remorse). Functions as +2, no sacred fire. Heavy and unresponsive.

**Cannot lift:** Purely evil, no redemptive motivation. Immovable as a mountain. No Strength check succeeds. Mephisto has not held this sword.

**Instant incineration:** Pure evil outsiders (demons, devils of sufficient power) touching the hilt: automatic Anathema Strike on contact.

---

## Additional Properties

**Flaming Illumination:** 60 ft. bright, reveals invisible within 30 ft., illusions translucent (DC 20 Perception to see through), evil/chaotic auras glow amber.

**Return to the Worthy:** If separated, slides along surfaces to nearest worthy wielder within 10 rounds. Opens doors. Moves through walls.

**Plane Travel (1/day):** Drive point-first into any surface, speak sincere prayer. Opens portal to any plane. 1 round/CL (min 10).

**Weight of Judgment (drawback):** After 24 hours: constant detect evil. After 7 days: emotional impressions from nearby sinners (DC 16 Will or −2 Perception/concentration). After 30 days: DC 20 Will each morning or compelled to confront greatest nearby evil. Ghost Rider and Zarathos are fully immune to all three stages.

---

## Campaign Use

Giving the sword to a player character is a significant decision. The Willing Blade and Weight of Judgment properties together ensure it is never a neutral power-up — it changes what the wielder sees, feels, and is drawn toward.

Suggested trigger: the sword appears — arrives, slides into the room, plants itself pommel-up at a PC's feet — when the party faces an evil that Ghost Rider alone cannot end. It chooses who it is offered to. It has never been wrong about the choice.
