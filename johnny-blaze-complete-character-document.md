# Johnny Blaze — Complete Character Document

![](images/Johnny_Blaze1.png)


> *"I made a deal with the devil. And the devil made a deal with something older than he is. Neither of us knew what we were getting into."*

---

## Who He Is

John Blaze is not a hero. He is not a villain. He is the man who is both. He sold his soul at seventeen to save a dying man — who died anyway, because Mephisto is Mephisto. He has spent every year since carrying a divine fire older than recorded history inside his chest, riding roads that never seem to end, and protecting people who will never know his name.

He holds nine Guinness World Records in motorcycle stunt riding. Doctor Strange has considered him a potential Sorcerer Supreme candidate. He has been to Hell three times. He walked out twice on his own. The third time, an angel opened the door.

He is approximately twenty-eight years old. He looks like he has been awake for twelve years. He smells faintly of brimstone in warm weather. His eyes glow orange-black when something evil is nearby, which is most of the time, and he learned to wear sunglasses indoors a long time ago.

He is the anchor for a being of divine judgment that predates the angelic-demonic divide. He is the reason that being doesn't burn everything it touches. He is, in some ways only he fully understands, the most important man alive.

He doesn't talk about it.

---

## Origins — The Deal at Seventeen

Johnny Blaze grew up in the Quentin Carnival — a traveling stunt show run by his father, Barton Blaze. His mother had already left. Crash Simpson, one of the carnival's performers, took Johnny in after his father's death. Johnny grew up eating exhaust fumes and learning to read a motorcycle the way other kids learned to read books.

He was seventeen when Crash Simpson was diagnosed with cancer.

Mephisto appeared to him — not with horns and a pitchfork, but as opportunity, as a whisper, as the thing that shows up when a desperate kid is alone with a problem that has no solution. The deal was simple: Johnny's soul in exchange for Crash's cure. Johnny signed.

Mephisto cured the cancer. Then Crash Simpson died in a stunt accident.

The technicality was the point. It always is, with Mephisto. He had the soul. He had the contract. He had Johnny Blaze. What he had not anticipated was the girl who had witnessed the deal — Roxanne Simpson, Crash's daughter — and the love she spoke aloud that diluted Mephisto's hold on Johnny's soul. What he had not anticipated was that Zarathos, the ancient empyreal spirit he had in his possession, was a being whose fundamental nature made him incompatible with Mephisto's agenda in ways that would only become clear over decades.

And what neither of them had anticipated was that the One Above All — the supreme authority of the Marvel cosmos, the being who physically wrestled the universe out of the hands of the Great Old Ones — would look at this arrangement and decide it was worth formalizing. The bond was sealed by the archangel Zadkiel. And then ratified above Zadkiel's authority.

Mephisto cannot alter the terms. He can exploit loopholes. He can lie about facts. He can use proxies. He cannot break the bond, steal Zarathos back, or directly override Johnny's will. He has tried all three. The first two he manages occasionally. The third has never worked once.

## Zarathos — The Ancient Fire

Zarathos is not a demon, though Mephisto kept him as one. He is something older — an entity that predates the separation between angels and demons, a being from the time when empyreal fire and demonic fire were the same thing and the distinction had not yet been made necessary by the war that created it.

He is at minimum 21,000 years old. He has had hosts on every inhabited continent. He has spoken every human language at some point. He built a cult of soul-worshippers among an ancient people, demanded their devotion, consumed their sins, and was eventually enslaved by Mephisto through the Centurious exploit — a soulless being used as a proxy, which stripped Zarathos of every power that required a soul to anchor to.

He spent millennia in Mephisto's service before being bound to Johnny Blaze.

The thing that nobody, including Zarathos himself, fully understood: being inside someone with a soul this strong, for this long, was changing him. Johnny's humanity seeped into him. His stubbornness, his refusal to quit, his particular and very human conviction that the innocent deserve protection — these are not Zarathos's native instincts. He acquired them. The line that defines what they have become is one he said himself, in a moment of clarity he probably wishes he could take back:

*"I never needed Blaze. I never needed him. I... I need him."*

He would not admit it again for a very long time. But it remains true, and they both know it.

Without a soul to anchor him, Zarathos begins to die. Without the tempering influence of Johnny's will, Ghost Rider becomes an extinction event pointed in random directions. They are, against every intention on both their parts, a functional partnership.

## Physical Description

**Height:** 5'10" (Ghost Rider form: 6'2" — supernatural growth on transformation)
**Weight:** 180 lbs
**Hair:** Blonde
**Eyes:** Blue — though they glow orange-black when evil is near, which is most of the time

He is lean and built the way professional riders are built: functional, durable, worn. His hands are calloused in the specific way of someone who has spent decades rebuilding engines by hand. He can identify a motorcycle make and model by sound from a hundred yards. He has not slept more than four consecutive hours since he was twenty-three.

His jacket is the Hell-Tempered Leather — black, scorched from within, impossible to damage with mundane weapons. It has never once been on fire and never once been torn. He has never been able to take it off. He has stopped trying. The leathers don't have a smell, exactly, but they have a quality — standing close to him, a person will feel the faint sense that the air around the jacket is remembering being on fire and deciding not to be, out of courtesy.

His eyes in full expression of the Burning Judgment Eyes are described as orange-black — orange at the center, consuming into black at the edges. Not orange-yellow like natural fire. Wrong in a way that suggests the fire is coming from somewhere that does not follow normal physics.

### Special Qualities

---

#### Burning Judgment Eyes (Su) — Constant

Zarathos bleeds through at the perceptual level even when Johnny is in full control. His eyes are always active — a perpetual, involuntary composite of **true seeing**, **detect evil**, **sin sight**, and **aura sight**, all running simultaneously and at all times. He cannot turn this off. He has tried. He stopped trying around year three.

What this means practically: Johnny Blaze always knows when something evil is in the room. He knows what kind. He can see through illusions, invisibility, and disguises. He can read the spiritual history of objects he touches. He knows, the moment he looks at you, whether you have harmed someone who did not deserve it.

He has learned to maintain eye contact anyway. This is its own kind of discipline.

The eyes glow orange-black. Not orange-yellow, like ordinary fire. Orange at the center, consuming into black at the edges, the color of fire that is being pulled toward something rather than pushing outward. In bright daylight this is subtle. In dim light it is unmistakable. In darkness, people can see his eyes before they can see him.

He keeps sunglasses in his jacket. Three pairs. He goes through them quickly.

**Mechanical effect:** Johnny is never surprised by evil creatures. He cannot be flanked by evil creatures. He automatically detects lies by evil creatures attempting to deceive him about supernatural matters. He may read the sin-history of objects as a full-round action (GM adjudication on specific content).

---

#### Hellfire Stigma (Su) — 10 ft. Radius, Always Active

Even in human form, Zarathos presses against the surface. Not enough to show, not enough to act — but enough to leak. Evil creatures entering or starting their turn within 10 feet of Johnny Blaze feel it before they see it: a sudden, sourceless certainty that they are being seen all the way through, and that whatever is looking does not like what it finds.

**Mechanical effect:** Evil creatures within 10 feet must succeed on a **DC 16 Will save** (soul-based, not mind-affecting; bypasses fear immunity for souled creatures) or be **shaken for 1 round**. Mindless creatures and constructs are immune. The genuinely innocent are immune. This triggers at the start of each such creature's turn.

The DC is 16 because Johnny is a CR 6 human. The aura is not his power — it is Zarathos's, leaking around the edges of containment. Ghost Rider's version of this effect operates at DC 30 and covers 60 feet. The 10-foot radius version is what Johnny produces when he is actively suppressing the transformation.

*In roleplay terms:* Most NPCs won't know why they want to stand further from the man in the black jacket. They just do. Some will feel it as guilt. Some will feel it as being watched. A few, the ones who have done particularly bad things, will feel something closer to recognition — a sudden awful awareness that something in the room knows, and that it remembers.

---

#### Nine World Records (Ex) — Always Active

Johnny Blaze holds nine Guinness World Records in motorcycle stunt riding. This is not talent. This is not even training, at this point. This is identity — a person who has spent more hours on a motorcycle than most people spend awake, who built his first bike at eleven, who can diagnose an engine problem by the sound it makes from two lanes over.

He has never been lost on a road in his life.

**Mechanical effect:**
- **+6 insight bonus to all Ride checks** (always on, automated on sheet)
- **+2 circumstance to Hell-Tempered Boots** stacks on top of the above for **+8 total** with boots equipped
- **Reroll once per scene** on any Ride check, taking the better result (track manually)
- **Drives any engine-powered vehicle** — cars, trucks, anything with a throttle — **at +2 without proficiency.** Folded into the +6 insight bonus on the sheet. The extra 2 points above base represent the preternatural ease with which he adapts to any machine with wheels.

---

#### Street Smarts (Ex) — Situational Buff

Johnny Blaze grew up in a traveling carnival. He spent his formative years in parking lots, truck stops, highway diners, and city back-alleys. He understands road culture, carnival culture, urban culture, and the particular ecosystem of people who live in neither one place nor another. He reads a crowd the way some people read text. He knows when someone is about to lie three seconds before they open their mouth.

He has never been lost on land. This is a separate fact from the motorcycle thing. He navigates by the position of the sun, the direction of traffic, the quality of the air, and something that might be Zarathos's 21,000 years of geography bleeding through at the edges.

**Mechanical effect (Buff item — activate in urban or road environments):**
- +4 untyped bonus to Bluff
- +4 untyped bonus to Perception
- +4 untyped bonus to Sense Motive

---

#### Occult Scholar (Ex) — Situational Buff

Doctor Strange has assessed Johnny Blaze as a potential Sorcerer Supreme candidate. This is not because Johnny has studied magic in any formal sense. It is because eleven years of carrying Zarathos has given him an experiential education in the cosmology of sin, judgment, and infernal mechanics that no library contains. He knows what a demon lord smells like when it is afraid. He knows what a contract looks like when it has been written with loopholes. He knows the difference between hellfire and dragonfire and holy fire — not academically, but physically, the way a doctor knows the difference between broken bones by the sound.

**Mechanical effect (Buff item — activate when making Knowledge checks about demons, infernal entities, contracts, or hellfire):**
- +4 insight bonus to Knowledge (arcana)
- +4 insight bonus to Knowledge (planes)
- +4 insight bonus to Knowledge (religion)

---

#### Hellfire Spark (Su) — Free Action

Johnny Blaze can produce a lighter-sized empyreal flame from his fingertips as a free action. It is orange-black. It is warm but does not burn what does not deserve to burn. It is just large enough to light a candle, ignite paper, or illuminate a small space for one round.

This does not require transformation. It does not require will or concentration. It happens the way breathing happens — it is simply the smallest amount of Zarathos that cannot be contained, expressed as light.

**Mechanical effect:** Functions as *prestidigitation* (fire applications only). 0 damage. Free action. Available at all times.

*Note: The flame is always orange-black. Not orange-yellow. Not red. Orange-black. If it is any other color, something is wrong.*

---

#### Infernal Contract Immunity (Ex/Su) — Passive + Situational

The bond between Johnny Blaze and Zarathos was sealed by the archangel Zadkiel. Then sealed above Zadkiel's authority by the One Above All. Mephisto cannot alter the terms. No Hell Lord can claim Zarathos. No infernal contract can bind Johnny's soul without his informed, uncoerced consent — and even then, the terms cannot supersede the ones already in place.

This has two mechanical expressions:

**Passive (automated on sheet):** +8 insight bonus to Will saves. This represents not wisdom, exactly, but the accumulated pattern recognition of someone who has been lied to by Mephisto specifically and by infernal entities generally for over a decade. He knows the shape of a trap.

**Hard Rule (no roll permitted):** Any attempt to alter Johnny's nature, his values, his contract terms, or his role as the Spirit of Vengeance automatically fails. There is no saving throw made. There is no opposed roll. The attempt simply does not work, regardless of the power level of the entity attempting it. Mephisto himself has failed this check. The One Above All sealed it. This is a narrative hard stop, not a mechanical challenge.

*In play terms:* If a player attempts to use magic, social pressure, mind control, or contract manipulation to make Johnny betray an innocent, abandon his role, or alter his fundamental character — the GM should inform them that the attempt fails, explain why, and move on. This is not a difficult conversation. It is baked into what he is.

---

#### Zarathos Bleedthrough — Passive Resistances (Ex/Su)

The longer the bond has existed, the more Zarathos's fundamental nature seeps into Johnny's human body. This is not transformation. It is environmental contamination — the edges of a divine fire warming the walls of the vessel that contains it.

**Mechanical effect (automated on sheet):**
- +4 untyped bonus to Will saves (always active, stacks with Iron Will, Indomitable Faith, etc.)
- Fire Resistance 5 (set manually in Foundry Traits tab)

**Buff items for additional resistances:**
- *Nerves of Steel (vs Supernatural Fear):* +2 trait bonus to Will vs. supernatural fear specifically. Activate manually when relevant.
- *Wisdom of Zarathos (vs Mind-Affecting):* +4 untyped to Fort and Ref saves vs. mind-affecting effects (charm, compulsion, fear, emotion, morale). Activate manually before relevant rolls.

---

#### Unbreakable Bond (Ex) — Passive

The One Above All sealed the bond. This has three consequences that cannot be undone by any entity at any power level:

1. **Johnny cannot be coerced into harming the genuinely innocent.** If a villain uses a hostage as a shield, Ghost Rider stops. Every time. Regardless of the stakes. This is not a limitation — it is the entire point.

2. **A loved one's name spoken within earshot requires a DC 22 Will save** or Johnny cannot attack that creature for 1 minute. This is Zarathos's weakness, not his. The name must be spoken by someone who loves Johnny sincerely, or by Johnny himself. This cannot be faked.

3. **Zarathos cannot be permanently transferred to another host without Johnny's willing consent.** Any attempt to steal, sell, or reassign the bond fails automatically. Mephisto cannot give Zarathos to someone else. This has been tried.

#### The Hell-Tempered Leathers

Johnny Blaze has one jacket. One pair of pants. One pair of boots. One belt. These are not separate pieces of gear — they are a set, and they have been part of him since the night of the first transformation.

The leathers were ordinary motorcycle gear until Zarathos wore them. The empyreal fire that passed through them that night changed their fundamental nature. They are black leather, scorched from within (not from without — the scorch pattern radiates outward from the inside, as if the fire is still moving). The stitching has never needed repair. The surface has never held a scratch. No mundane weapon has ever marked them.

They cannot be removed while the bond is active. Johnny stopped trying to take the jacket off around year two. He suspects they would vanish on transformation and reappear on return, the way certain things do when they are fundamentally part of what he is.

#### The Hellfire Shotguns — Two Weapons, One Power

Johnny carries two shotguns. Both appear when he needs them. He reaches behind his back and they are there. There is no sheath on his back — there has never been a sheath. They manifest the way the Hellcycle does: the world accommodates what he needs, because the power inside him has decided this is how it works.

Both weapons are inert in anyone else's hands — ordinary shotguns, no glow, no empyreal fire, no fear effects. The power is in Blaze. The guns are conduits.

Both carry **18 rounds of physical ammunition**: 12 brass-cased shells in bandoliers (6 per side) and 6 in the magazine tube. The casings are heat-treated and flame-proof. When Johnny or Ghost Rider fires them, empyreal rounds conjure themselves — the physical shells are courtesy, not necessity. Track the 18 rounds for anyone else who picks them up. A box of 25 additional brass-cased shells rides in his pocket as backup.

##### Winchester Model 1887 — The Mare's Leg

**How it became what it is:** The comics origin is Ghost Rider Vol. 3, 1991 — Danny Ketch's Ghost Rider grabbed an ordinary shotgun during a fight, and hellfire passed through the barrel. The Winchester form (lever-action, sawed-off) comes from the 2007 film — the version given to Johnny by Carter Slade, the Caretaker. Both origins are true in the spirit of the character. The power was always there. The gun is just the shape it learned to take.

## Background Details — The Life Between Transformations

Johnny Blaze lives on the road. He has not had a permanent address in years. He works stunt shows when he can find them, takes mechanic work when he can't, and occasionally takes on the kind of jobs that come to people who have a reputation for solving problems that conventional authorities can't touch.

He is not wealthy. He is rarely broke. He keeps enough to move, enough to eat, enough to keep the bike running, and not much else. Possessions are complicated when you live the way he lives. He has learned to want less.

He does not sleep well. This is partly the nature of the bond — Zarathos does not sleep, and something of that wakefulness bleeds through. He manages four hours most nights, sometimes less. He has been doing this long enough that four hours feels normal.

He has a coffee problem. Not as flavor — as function. Six cups minimum to achieve anything resembling alertness. He is deeply, specifically opinionated about gas station coffee (acceptable) versus diner coffee (preferable) versus urban coffee shop coffee (fine but they look at his eyes funny and he has to explain the sunglasses again).

He can build a motorcycle from parts in a weekend. He has done this in parking lots, in barns, in the lobbies of abandoned motels. He knows the sound every engine makes when it needs something and what specifically it needs. This is not a professional skill, at this point. It is more like a language he was born speaking.

He does not discuss the deal unless he has to. He does not discuss Zarathos unless he has to. He does not discuss what he sees when the Burning Judgment Eyes are working, which is always, because there is no version of that conversation that ends well for anyone involved.

He is deeply, genuinely kind to animals. They sense what he is and they should run. Most of them do. The ones who don't — the strays, the old ones, the ones that have already had their capacity for fear beaten out of them — he is patient with. He has occasionally been seen sharing his food with dogs in parking lots, talking to them in a low voice, not about anything in particular. He does not explain this behavior. It does not require explanation.

He has been to Hell three times:
1. He went to reclaim something. He succeeded. He walked out under his own power.
2. He was taken. He walked out under his own power, through a door Mephisto had not left open.
3. He went because there was no other option. An angel opened the door on the way out. He has not discussed the details of that trip with anyone.

## The Transformation

![](images/Johnny_Blaze2_hellcycle.png)


Transformation is a standard action under ordinary circumstances. Under the following conditions, it becomes an immediate action — it happens whether Johnny chooses it or not:

- Mortal danger witnessed in progress
- Innocent blood spilled within 30 feet
- True evil of sufficient magnitude within his perceptual range

The third condition is the most complicated, because the Burning Judgment Eyes give him an enormous perceptual range and the world is full of evil. He has gotten better at not triggering involuntarily in cities. It took years. He still sometimes has to pull over on a highway because something in the direction of a certain town is bad enough that Zarathos starts pressing against the inside of his skull.

When the transformation happens:

The leathers don't change — they are already what they are. His height increases four inches. The flesh burns away — not painfully, not slowly, but all at once, the way a magician reveals what was always underneath. The skull is revealed: human-shaped, jaw intact, eye sockets blazing with orange-black hellfire that streams backward and upward like the flame knows which way up is and chooses to ignore it. The fire does not flicker. It presses outward.

The plants within thirty feet grey at the edges. Water steams. Animals leave before it finishes. People who have not sinned significantly feel uneasy without knowing why. People who have feel something considerably more specific.

The Hellcycle arrives. It responds to his will — it can come from whatever surface he designates, appearing from a barrel fired at the ground, or simply materializing at the edge of his attention. It does not need to be summoned if it is already nearby. It comes.

Ghost Rider does not speak the way Johnny speaks. The voice is different — lower, with a resonance that does not come from a throat. He is precise. He does not have Johnny's tendency toward dark humor. He rarely explains himself. He is, in general, doing the same things Johnny would do, but without the editorial running commentary.

## The Zarathos Dial — Three Modes of Operation

Ghost Rider is not a fixed state. He is a spectrum. Where Johnny and Zarathos have reached equilibrium in any given moment determines what you get.

**Blaze Leading (CR 17)** — Johnny is in control. This is Ghost Rider at his most deliberate, most merciful. He suppresses the Hellfire Aura near innocents instinctively. He uses targeted abilities over area effects. He offers chances to flee. He will not kill someone who surrenders. He is still terrifying, but in the way that a judge is terrifying — authority, precision, and the unsettling sense that there is nowhere to run from a verdict that has already been reached.

**Composite Default (CR 19)** — The balance. Zarathos wants to burn things. Johnny wants to be precise. The result is controlled ferocity. Ghost Rider is fully capable in this mode, uses his complete toolkit against guilty targets, does not hold back — but he is not indiscriminate. He is doing what he came here to do, and he knows exactly what that is.

**Zarathos Surging (CR 21)** — Zarathos is winning the argument. This occurs when the evil is overwhelming, when something genuinely horrifying has happened, or when Johnny fails a DC 22 Will save at a moment of trauma. The Hellfire Aura cannot be suppressed. The Hellfire Nova becomes 15d6 with no save. Cold Hellfire is used without hesitation. The Aura of Holy Fire activates automatically. Zarathos is not thinking about collateral damage. He is thinking about fire.

*This mode should feel like an escalation that the party is relieved to see — and slightly worried by.*

## Cosmological Position

Johnny Blaze is not a hero in the four-color sense. He is not a villain. He is something older and more specific than either: a commissioned instrument of divine judgment who chose, at seventeen, to take on more than any human being should have to carry, and who has been carrying it without setting it down for over a decade.

The One Above All — who created the universe, who wrestled it away from the Great Old Ones before recorded history — looked at the arrangement between a stunt rider, a corrupt Hell Lord, and an ancient divine fire, and decided it was worth formalizing. This is not a small thing. Gods of considerable power move carefully around Ghost Rider because of this. Mephisto, who is not careful about anything, is specifically careful about this.

The empyreal fire Zarathos carries is not evil. It is not good. It is judgment — a force that operates entirely outside the moral axis that organizes conventional cosmology. It burns the guilty. It passes through the innocent. It does not negotiate, does not compromise, and does not apologize. Johnny Blaze, the man who carries it, has all of those qualities in abundance, and the tension between the two of them is, in many ways, the entire character.

Doctor Strange has said that if Johnny Blaze ever fully mastered the power he carries, the result would be something the Marvel cosmos has no name for.

Johnny's opinion on this, when pressed, is approximately: *"I'm just trying to make it to next week."*

He means it. That is, somehow, the most reassuring and most terrifying thing about him.
