# Zarathos — Spirit of Vengeance

![](images/Zarathos.png)

> *"When Zarathos takes possession of the Ghost Rider, the Ghost Rider's powers are, for most intents and purposes, boundless and godlike."*
> — Doctor Strange

---

## Canon

Zarathos is 21,000 years old at minimum. He predates the angelic-demonic divide — he was an angel of justice, a Spirit of Vengeance, sent to protect the world of men, before the concepts of "angel" and "demon" had fully separated in the cosmic hierarchy. He is older than Mephisto's current form. He is older than most of the named Demon Lords. He remembers when the rules were still being written.

Mephisto coveted Zarathos's power. Using a soulless creature as a proxy (Zarathos's one weakness — he cannot affect the soulless), Mephisto trapped him, stole his memories, and drove him mad across millennia of imprisonment in human hosts. Each generation, a new Rider. Each Rider, a new war between the fire and the soul that carries it. The Host keeps Zarathos leashed. Zarathos keeps the Host alive. Neither particularly enjoys the arrangement.

Zarathos unbound is not evil in the way a demon is evil. He is *amoral* in the way a wildfire is amoral. He is vengeance without mercy — the mechanism, not the motive. He targets the most guilty creature in range. Everything else is scenery. A pickpocket gets slapped. A murderer gets the full treatment. A soul-eating demon gets torn apart with bare hands while Zarathos quotes scripture in a language that hasn't been spoken in ten thousand years. He might decide the party's fight isn't important and walk three blocks toward the *real* evil — the one sacrificing virgins in a basement nobody knew about.

His Wisdom is intentionally low for his CR tier. Demon Lords typically have WIS 29–34. Zarathos has WIS 24. He is primordial destructive force, not wisdom or judgment. This is the weakness Mephisto exploited: Zarathos can be tricked, trapped, redirected. He was enslaved using a soulless creature because he couldn't perceive the trap. He is the ancient fire. He is not the ancient *mind*.

In canon, Zarathos possesses 21 millennia of hosts' languages (basis for Tongues), has been to Hell and walked out, has fought cosmic-tier entities to a standstill, and was described by Doctor Strange — a man who has seen everything — as possessing power that is "boundless and godlike." Johnny Blaze briefly served as King of Hell using Zarathos's authority. The fire is not a metaphor.

## PF1E Adaptation

Zarathos exists in two forms in this project: the CR 26 NPC stat block (below) and the surge form for the leveling class (in the guidebook, Part 5).

**Inheritance chain:** Zarathos inherits ALL abilities from Ghost Rider (CR 19) and Johnny Blaze (CR 6). Chain weapon feats are NOT inherited — Zarathos uses claws and natural attacks. All special qualities, knowledge skills, and fear mechanics are inherited and enhanced.

**No stat block for the leveling class surge.** The user directed: "He's the big brother of all demon lords, empyreal lords, and Great Old Ones. He can open any size can of whoop-ass. He IS Justice incarnate." In the leveling class, Zarathos is as strong as the scene requires. The DM takes full control. No numbers. The CR 26 stat block exists for campaigns where Zarathos appears as an NPC or antagonist.

**Empyreal descriptor.** All Zarathos damage carries [empyreal]. Bypasses all fire/holy/unholy resistance. Same descriptor as Ghost Rider's hellfire and the Sword of Uri-El's sacred fire — they are all the same fundamental energy.

### Immunities & Defenses

Immune: fire, empyreal (self), mind-affecting, death effects, poison, soul drain, energy drain.
Resist: acid 30, cold 30, electricity 30.
Weakness: soul-trap artifacts (DC 30 Will or imprisoned); soulless creatures suppress his power.

### Three Auras (Always Active)

**Aura of Ancient Dread (Su) — 120 ft.** DC 35 Will, soul-based. Full tier table in Existential Dread reference. Adds Primordial Wrongness: Tier 0 creatures suffer −2 attacks/skills, no save.

**Hellfire Aura (Su) — 30 ft.** 8d6 empyreal/round, DC 35 Ref half. Cannot be suppressed. Fire-immune creatures still take 4d6 empyreal (soul component).

**Smoldering Presence (Su) — 60 ft.** Plants die. Water evaporates. Fires gutter. Animals flee 120 ft. No damage, no save.

### Attacks

**Melee:** 2 claws +45/+40 (4d6+21 plus 4d6 empyreal, 19–20) and bite +42 (2d8+19 plus soul rend)
**Power Attack:** Claws +37/+32 (4d6+35 plus 4d6 empyreal)
**Ranged:** Hellfire blast +30 touch (12d8 empyreal, 60 ft.)

### Signature Abilities

**Penance Stare + Judgment Lock:** DC 40 Will; 5d4 Wis/round on fail, 1 Wis flat on success. At Wis 0: catatonic, soul consumed.

**Hellfire Nova (1/day):** 10d6 empyreal, 30 ft. radius, DC 35 Ref half.

**Brimstone Impact (1/day):** 20d6 empyreal + 10d6 bludgeoning, 30 ft. radius, DC 35 Ref half.

**Soul Rend (Su):** Bite attack, DC 37 Fort or 2d6 CON damage and 1d6 Wis damage. On kill: soul consumed (no resurrection short of divine intervention).

**Cold Hellfire Touch (3/day):** 4d6 Wis damage, DC 35 Fort half. No physical damage. Burns the soul only.

**Soul Binding (Su):** As Ghost Rider's chain version but as a standalone ability — no weapon required. DC 35 Fort.

## Leveling Class — Zarathos Surge

In the leveling class, Zarathos surge is triggered by: overburn failure, 0 HP while transformed, d100 entry #1, overwhelming evil/trauma (DC 22 + souls stored), or Medallion at capacity (DC 15 + Medallion AC bonus).

**The DM takes full control.** Size → Large. +4 STR, +2 CON, +4 CHA on top of Rider's Mantle. Heaven's Brand increases one die step. Gains claws and bite. Duration: 1d4+1 rounds, DC 20 Will to end each round after.

**Aftermath:** Reverts to Host form. Exhausted 10 minutes. Cannot transform 1 hour. Medallion emptied — all souls consumed. All overburn damage hits at once.

At level 19 (Unbound): overburn no longer triggers surge. At level 20 (Spirit Unchained): voluntary surge with shared DM/player control.
