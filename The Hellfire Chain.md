# The Hellfire Chain

![](images/Hellfire_Chain.png)

> *It is not a weapon. It is an argument. One that always wins.*

---

## Overview

The Hellfire Chain is not carried. It exists when Ghost Rider needs it and does not exist when he does not. It coils at his hip, draped over his shoulder, pooled at his feet — wherever it needs to be. It responds to mental commands. It has no fixed length. Marvel Database describes the upper bound of its length as "unknown." This is accurate. It is as long as it needs to be.

It is a +4 anarchic flaming burst spiked chain that deals **empyreal damage** rather than fire damage. The empyreal descriptor means it bypasses all fire resistance, fire immunity, holy resistance, and unholy resistance without exception. SR still applies.

Proficiency with the chain is **inherent to Ghost Rider's divine office**. No feat required. No training involved. The chain is part of what he is.

## Soul Binding (Su)

**DC 30 Fort — triggers on any successful chain strike. Negates binding, not damage.**

On a failed save, the target is soul-bound:

- **Paralyzed** — soul pinned to the material plane, body cannot act against the chain's will
- **Cannot teleport, dimension door, plane shift, ethereal jaunt, or use any magical movement** — the soul is anchored
- **−8 penalty** to all concentration and caster level checks
- **3d6 empyreal damage per round** automatically while bound
- **All magical enhancements suppressed** as per *greater dispel magic* (CL 18, automatic — no check)

**Breaking free:** DC 38 Strength check (full-round action) OR *break enchantment* / *freedom* at CL 30+ OR artifact-level magic. Standard magic does not work. This binds the soul, not the body.

*Note: This is not a grapple. No CMB/CMD is involved. The soul is pinned to reality itself.*

---

## Chain Whip (Ranged Touch Attack)

Ghost Rider can lash the chain at range as a ranged touch attack (+22 touch, 30 ft.) rather than a standard melee attack. This version does not deal the base chain damage — instead, it establishes Soul Binding contact (same DC 30 Fort) and deals **3d6 empyreal damage per round** while the chain remains in contact. Ghost Rider does not need to maintain the chain physically — it responds to his will.

---

## Lunge

With the Lunge feat, Ghost Rider can extend the chain's effective reach to **15 ft.** as a standard option (−2 AC penalty applies). Combined with Combat Reflexes (DEX +4 = 5 AoOs per round), the chain threatens a massive area and punishes any movement through the threatened zone.

---

## Canon Notes

The Hellfire Chain originates in the comics as a standard motorcycle chain that absorbed empyreal fire during Ghost Rider's first transformation — it became a permanent expression of Zarathos's power. In the FASERIP system it is simply listed as a +4 equivalent weapon with soul-trapping properties. The PF1E implementation mechanizes the soul-trapping as Soul Binding and uses the spiked chain as the closest published weapon chassis.

The chain responds to mental commands and can be used in ways that no physical chain could — wrapping around corners, changing length mid-swing, coiling around a target independently. At the table, treat these as flavor that does not require additional mechanics beyond what is listed above.

---

## The Zarathos Version

Zarathos does **not** carry the chain. He uses natural attacks (claws, bite) and the Sword of Uri-El. The chain is Ghost Rider's weapon — it belongs to the composite form, not to either component alone.

If Zarathos wishes to bind something, he uses Soul Binding (Su) directly — an action rather than a weapon strike.
