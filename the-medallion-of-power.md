# The Medallion of Power

![](images/Medallion_of_Power.png)

> *"Long ago, the Medallion of Power was created by the cult known as The Blood to house the entities known as the Spirits of Vengeance. Due to the power it held, the Medallion and its Spirits were coveted by the ancient demon Mephisto."*

---

## Canon

The Medallion of Power is one of the oldest artifacts in the Ghost Rider mythos. It was forged by the ancient cult known as The Blood — a human order dedicated to the Spirits of Vengeance — as a housing for the Spirits themselves. The Medallion is not merely a container. It is a bridge between the mortal and the divine, the mechanism by which a human soul can host something as vast and ancient as Zarathos without being instantly incinerated.

Mephisto coveted the Medallion because it was the key to controlling the Spirits. With it, he could bind Zarathos to human hosts. Without it, the Spirits were free agents — divine judgment answering to no one but the highest authority. The Medallion's power made it simultaneously the instrument of Zarathos's imprisonment and the source of the Host's supernatural resilience. It is the reason Ghost Rider can take hits that would destroy a mortal body. It is the reason the bond between Host and Spirit holds at all.

In canon, the Medallion adapts its physical form to the Host — gas cap on a motorcycle, pendant around a neck, brand on a chest, coin in a pocket. It cannot be removed by force. It is part of the Host in the same way a soul is part of a person. When the Host transforms, the Medallion burns with empyreal fire. When the Host is human, it is warm to the touch and faintly unsettling to anyone who holds it.

The Medallion's role in our build is extrapolated from canon. In the comics, it is primarily a narrative object — the thing that explains *why* the bond works. We mechanized it as a soul battery: a tactical resource system that creates constant decision pressure. Every soul banked is armor gained and danger increased. Every soul spent is a crisis averted and protection lost.

---

## PF1E Adaptation

The Medallion activates at level 8. Before that, the Host is running on raw willpower and the Spirit's baseline power. The Medallion deepens the bond and unlocks soul storage — the ability to capture and hold the souls of the evil dead for both passive defense and active emergency spends.

**Key adaptation decisions:**

**Soul battery, not stat stick.** The Medallion's original draft included DR, SR, and Channel Resistance — none of which have canon basis. All fabricated effects were cut. The Medallion is a soul battery. It stores souls. Those souls provide passive AC and can be spent for emergency effects. That's it.

**Defense must be earned.** The AC bonus requires killing evil creatures and banking their souls. You can't buy it, enchant it, or fake it. Every point of sacred AC represents a judgment rendered and a soul collected.

**The tension is the point.** Every soul banked raises the stakes. Full Medallion = surge pressure every round of combat. Every soul spent removes protection but relieves pressure. The player is constantly choosing: hoard for defense, or spend for survival? That agonizing IS the gameplay.

## Mechanics

**Physical form:** Adapts to Host — gas cap, pendant, brand, coin. Cannot be removed, sold, or destroyed short of artifact-level intervention.

**Harvest:** Swift action on an evil kill while transformed. Penance auto-feeds at Wis 0 if space available.

### Soul Storage and AC

| Level | Capacity | Sacred AC Bonus |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 8 | 3 | +2 |
| 12 | 5 | +3 |
| 16 | 7 | +4 |
| 20 | 10 | +5 |

AC bonus granted when Medallion is at capacity.

### Active Spends (Immediate Action — Soul Released to Afterlife)

| Cost | Name | Effect |
|------|------|--------|
| 1 | Deny the Reaper | Reroll failed save |
| 1 | Soulshield | Temp HP = 2× level |
| 1 | Soul Deflection | Negate confirmed crit |
| 1 | Hellfire Retort | Next attack +2d6 empyreal |
| 1 | Shatter Binding | End compulsion/charm/possession on self or adjacent ally |
| 2 | Cheat Death | At 0 HP, drop to 1 HP instead. Does NOT trigger Zarathos surge. |

### Capacity Trigger

At maximum soul capacity, the Host must succeed on a **Will save (DC 15 + current Medallion AC bonus)** each round of combat or Zarathos surges.

| Level | AC Bonus | DC | Will | Success Rate |
|-------|----------|----|------|-------------|
| 8 | +2 | 17 | +6 | **50%** |
| 12 | +3 | 18 | +8 | **55%** |
| 16 | +4 | 19 | +10 | **60%** |
| 20 | +5 | 20 | +12 | **65%** |

Spending even one soul below capacity removes the trigger entirely.

### Overburn Interaction

Each soul stored adds +1 to overburn DCs. Full Medallion = more dangerous overburn. This is separate from the capacity trigger — both can apply simultaneously.

### Surge Aftermath

On Zarathos surge, the Medallion is **emptied**. All souls consumed by Zarathos. All bonuses gone.

## Durability

Suppressible by Dispel/Antimagic (dormant until suppression ends). Breakable by artifact-level force (Zarathos repairs in 1d4 days). Not permanently destroyable short of divine intervention.

Spend a soul, drop below capacity, and the trigger stops. That's the loop.*
