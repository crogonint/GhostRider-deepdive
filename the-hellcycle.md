# The Hellcycle

![](images/Hellcycle1.png)

> *"The bike hasn't needed gas in three months. Last Tuesday it started itself. He's trying not to think about it."*

---

## Canon

The Hellcycle was not always supernatural. It was Johnny Blaze's personal motorcycle — a beat-up chopper, nothing special. The night Zarathos first possessed him, the transformation of Johnny's body also transformed his bike. Empyreal fire flooded through the frame, the engine, the wheels. By morning it was something that no longer obeyed the laws of physics and never would again.

A mechanic examining the bike determines it is mechanically impossible. It has no fuel system. The engine block does not match any known make. The tires are made of something that is not rubber. It does not require fuel, maintenance, air, or a road. It has been depicted outpacing a thrown Mjolnir in straight-line travel — its true speed is, in the strictest sense, unlimited.

The wheels burn with empyreal flame at all times when Ghost Rider is transformed. Anything making physical contact with the wheels is burned — not by heat, but by judgment. At speed, the Hellcycle leaves a Trail of Hellfire: a wall of empyreal fire, 10 ft. wide, 15 ft. tall, persisting for one minute. Nothing short of flight or teleportation escapes it, and at 15 ft. tall, even low-flying opponents find themselves banking into flame.

The Hellcycle is semi-sentient (INT 8). It comes when called. It has been seen driving itself through walls to reach Ghost Rider. If destroyed, it vanishes in black smoke and reforms at full HP within 1d10 rounds. While Ghost Rider is bound to a Host, the Hellcycle cannot be permanently destroyed. Period.

![](images/Hellcycle2.png)

In Johnny's human form, it is an ordinary (if supernaturally menacing) motorcycle. Fast, responsive, eerily reliable — but no hellfire wheels, no trail, no supernatural movement. It is a very good bike that happens to have no fuel system and an engine that shouldn't work.

![](images/Johnny's_1969_Raodster.png)

The Hellcycle is, in a sense, the third party in the relationship between Host and Spirit. It is the physical expression of Ghost Rider's freedom, speed, and reach. Without it, the Rider is a devastating but relatively static combatant. On it, he becomes a pursuit engine that cannot be outrun, that leaves walls of fire behind it, and that can cross a continent in hours.

## PF1E Adaptation

Two versions exist: the CR 19 NPC's Hellcycle (full supernatural vehicle) and the leveling class's Infernal Steed (a mount or vehicle that gains upgrades as the Host levels).

**Key adaptation decisions:**

**No flight.** The Infernal Steed does not fly. It jumps. It drives up walls. It crosses water. But it does not sprout wings. This is canon — the Hellcycle is not an airplane. Hellfire Leap (level 14) gives eyebrow-raising vertical mobility without breaking positioning the way permanent flight does. The CR 19 NPC version treats aerial jumps as GM-adjudicated parabolic arcs — no hard cap, because Ghost Rider has been depicted launching off buildings and arcing across city blocks.

**Mount is defensive, not offensive.** The mount does not channel empyreal damage through its own attacks. Things that HIT the mount take Hellfire Backlash damage (level 18+), but the mount's claws/hooves/wheels deal normal damage only. This prevents Griffon pounce stacking (3 attacks × free empyreal = broken).

**Stacking.** The Infernal Steed upgrades stack with the base mount's existing abilities. If the player chose a Griffon, it keeps fly and pounce. If they chose a motorcycle, it keeps Thundering Crash. The class adds on top.

**No pounce off a Leap.** Hellfire Leap is a move action, not a charge. A mount cannot pounce at the end of a Leap.

## CR 19 NPC — Hellcycle

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Size | Large object |
| Hardness | 20 (infernal iron) |
| HP | 120 |
| Intelligence | 8 (semi-sentient) |
| Tactical speed | 120 ft. (all terrain, walls, water) |
| True speed | Supernatural/unlimited (~500 mph overland) |
| Immunities | Fire, critical hits, death effects, mind-affecting |
| Regen | 5 hp/round while Ghost Rider is transformed |
| If destroyed | Reforms in 1d10 rounds within 10 miles |
| Recall | Standard action, 1d4 rounds, 10-mile range |

**Hellfire Wheels (Su):** 4d6 empyreal on any physical contact with wheels.

**Trail of Hellfire:** Active at speed 60+ ft. 10 ft. wide × 15 ft. tall, 6d6 empyreal (DC 25 Ref half), persists 1 minute.

**Rampage (Full-round):** Straight-line path up to 120 ft., targeting all creatures in path. Primary target: +28 melee, 4d6+10 bludgeoning + 4d6 empyreal. Secondary targets: +22, same damage. DC 24 Fort or prone.

**Bonded Feats (automatic while mounted):** Mounted Combat, Ride-By Attack, Spirited Charge, Trample. No Ride check ever required.

**Human Form:** Speed 60 ft., HP 30, Hardness 5, no supernatural properties. Looks like a beat-up 1969 chopper. Is not.
