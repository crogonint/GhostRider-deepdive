# The Hellfire Shotgun
## Artifact / Divine Weapon — Stat Blocks

---

## Canon Origin

**Comics (Ghost Rider vol. 3 #14, June 1991):**
Johnny Blaze was carrying an ordinary shotgun during a confrontation with Danny Ketch's
Ghost Rider. When Ghost Rider grabbed the weapon, his hellfire passed through it and
permanently transformed it into a hellfire-channeling artifact. The power was later
revealed to emanate from within Johnny himself — the gun is a conduit for Blaze's own
suppressed hellfire potential, not an independent power source. This is why it works
specifically and unusually well in his hands and becomes supercharged in Ghost Rider's.

**Film (Ghost Rider, 2007):**
A Winchester Model 1887 lever-action shotgun given to Johnny by Carter Slade (the
Caretaker) before the final confrontation with Blackheart. Johnny thrust the weapon into
a shadow and it transformed — the hellfire within him channeling up through the steel
and igniting the shells. Visual basis for all subsequent depictions.

**Unlimited ammunition:** Confirmed across both continuities. The gun conjures its own
empyreal rounds — it cannot be emptied.

---

## Physical Description

![](images/Hellfire_Shotgun.png)


A sawed-off Winchester Model 1887 lever-action shotgun, twelve-gauge, with the barrel
cut to approximately fourteen inches. The wood of the stock is scorched black from the
inside out — not burned, but *changed*, as if the grain of the wood reorganized itself
around something that no longer cares about being wood. The metal of the receiver and
barrel is similarly darkened, and the lever gleams with a faint orange-black luminescence
that pulses slightly when held by Blaze.

When unfired, it looks like a battered, ancient firearm with an unsettling warmth to the
metal. When fired, the muzzle blast is not orange or yellow — it is the same orange-black
as Ghost Rider's eyes, and it smells of brimstone and scorched parchment rather than
propellant. In Ghost Rider's hands the transformation is immediate and total: the wood
chars further and splits away, the metal runs with lines of hellfire along the grain like
veins, and the barrel glows as if fresh from the forge. It is clearly no longer a gun. It
is a divine instrument shaped like one.

## Special Properties

**Soul-Based Fear (Su)**
Any creature struck by the Hellfire Shotgun must succeed on a **DC 16 Will save**
or be shaken for 1 round. This effect is soul-based, not mind-affecting. It bypasses
immunity to fear and immunity to mind-affecting effects for any creature possessing a
soul. Mindless creatures (constructs, oozes, mindless undead, plants, vermin with no
INT score) are immune.

**Evil's Bane**
Against evil-aligned creatures, the Hellfire Shotgun deals an additional +1d6 empyreal
damage. Against demons specifically, this increases to +2d6 empyreal damage.

**Hellfire Revelation (Su)**
When the Hellfire Shotgun strikes a creature that has committed specific acts of harm
against innocents, the target must succeed on a **DC 16 Will save** or briefly relive
one of those acts (GM's choice or randomly determined from known history). This is
not the full Penance Stare — it is a flash, a fragment, a taste. The target is shaken
1d4 rounds on a failed save, regardless of whether the first fear save was made.
Against a target immune to the soul-based fear effect (Tier 0), this property also
does not function.

**Motorcycle Conjuration (Su, 1/day)**
Johnny can point the Hellfire Shotgun at any empty road or surface and conjure a
flaming motorcycle (treat as Hell Cycle, Johnny's stats, no supernatural movement
abilities — mundane conjuration only). The cycle persists 1 hour or until destroyed.
*Comics-accurate ability, rarely invoked.*

**Inert Without Blaze**
The Hellfire Shotgun is keyed to Johnny Blaze's soul. In any other creature's hands,
it functions as a non-magical sawed-off shotgun (1d8 lethal, 20 ft. range increment,
normal proficiency requirements). It will not fire empyreal rounds, will not trigger
fear effects, and will not glow. The Scarlet Witch exception (comics continuity) was
a temporary post-Empirikul effect and is not replicated here.

## Upgrade Path

When Johnny Blaze transforms into Ghost Rider, the Hellfire Shotgun does not simply
become more powerful — it *completes*. The weapon was always Ghost Rider's. It was
channeling a fraction of his full fire through a human intermediary. When the Rider
takes hold, it stops being a gun that happens to fire hellfire and becomes a divine
instrument that happens to be shaped like a gun.

**See Stat Block B for Ghost Rider's version.**

## Special Properties

**Soul-Based Fear — Upgraded (Su)**
DC **30** Will save (matches Ghost Rider's Existential Dread aura DC) on any creature
struck. Soul-based, not mind-affecting. Tier effects as per the Aura of Existential
Dread — the shotgun blast is a focused delivery of the same dread that the aura
broadcasts passively. A creature already affected by the aura does not save again
— the blast simply extends the duration of its current condition by 1d4 rounds.

**Hellfire Revelation — Full (Su)**
In Ghost Rider's hands, the brief flash becomes a sustained burn. Any creature struck
must succeed on a **DC 30 Will save** or relive 1d4 significant acts of harm it has
committed, simultaneously and in rapid succession (not the totality of the Penance
Stare — more like a warning shot of the soul). Failed save: Frightened 1d4 rounds.
Successful save: Shaken 1 round. *This stacks with the Soul-Based Fear effect — a
creature that fails both saves is Panicked, not merely Frightened.*

**Evil's Bane — Full (Su)**
+2d6 empyreal against evil-aligned creatures.
+4d6 empyreal against demons and devil-kind.
+6d6 empyreal against demon lords, archdevils, and entities of equivalent tier.
*A demon lord struck by Ghost Rider's Hellfire Shotgun takes 6d6 + 6d6 = 12d6
empyreal damage per barrel — before any other modifiers.*

**Point-Blank Soul Blast (Su)**
Within 10 ft., Ghost Rider can fire both barrels simultaneously as a standard action
in a focused soul-burn rather than physical blast. The target takes 8d6 empyreal
damage (Ref DC 30 half) and must make a DC 30 Will save or be affected as if they
had failed the initial contact check for the Penance Stare — they are immediately
subject to Judgment Lock if Ghost Rider chooses to maintain gaze. *This is the
"I could do this the hard way or the harder way" option.*

**Supercharged Motorcycle Conjuration (Su, 3/day)**
Ghost Rider can conjure a full Hell Cycle (with all supernatural movement properties)
as a swift action by firing one barrel at the ground. The cycle persists until
dismissed. This is the comics-accurate ability — Danny Ketch's hellfire imprinted
the motorcycle-conjuration ability on the weapon permanently.

**Indestructible in Ghost Rider's Grip**
While held by Ghost Rider or Zarathos, the weapon cannot be sundered, disarmed
against Ghost Rider's will, or destroyed by any non-artifact means. It can be taken
from a stunned, unconscious, or incapacitated Ghost Rider but will return to his hand
(teleport, instantaneous, free action) when he regains consciousness or wills it.

## Usage Notes

**Action Economy**
In Johnny's hands: the Winchester fires 2 shots per action round (lever-action). The Remington fires 4 shots per action round (semi-automatic). Empyreal rounds conjure themselves — no reload required for Blaze. Others must track the 18 physical rounds (12 bandoliers + 6 in tube) per weapon.

In Ghost Rider's hands: both weapons fire at their respective rates — 2/round for the Winchester, 4/round for the Remington — with free-action empyreal reload. Ghost Rider can discharge the Remington four times in a full-attack round while still having his chain available as a separate weapon. The shotguns and chain are functionally independent weapon systems. A typical full-attack round: **chain +32/+27/+22/+17, or Remington +22/+17/+12/+7**. In practice he chooses one per round based on whether range or reach is more useful.

**Against the Penance Stare**
The Point-Blank Soul Blast is a tactical option when eye contact cannot be established
— a being that keeps its eyes averted or closed to avoid the Stare can still be hit
by the shotgun's soul-burn, which forces the same DC check without requiring gaze.
It does not trigger the full Judgment Lock (that still requires eye contact) but it
is a meaningful degradation option that whittles down a target's will before
the Stare is eventually established.
