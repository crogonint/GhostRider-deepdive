# The Hellcycle — Ghost Rider's Infernal Motorcycle

![](images/Hellcycle1.png)

![](images/Hellcycle2.png)


> [!abstract] Vehicle Overview
> The Hellcycle is a semi-sentient infernal vehicle bonded to Ghost Rider through Zarathos. It is not a separate creature — it does not grant XP independently — but it functions as a physical extension of Ghost Rider's power the same way a paladin's mount functions as an extension of divine grace. In Johnny Blaze's human form, it is an ordinary (if supernaturally menacing) motorcycle. In Ghost Rider form, it becomes an instrument of divine judgment with capabilities that are, in the strictest sense, uncapped.

> [!danger] Combat Speed vs. True Speed
> **Tactical combat speed:** 120 ft. per round. Use this for all encounter mechanics.
>
> **True supernatural speed:** Effectively unlimited. The Hellcycle can outpace a thrown Mjolnir in straight-line travel. For campaign overland purposes: treat as **500 miles per hour** with no fuel, no maintenance, and no terrain penalty. It can cross a continent in hours. It can outrun any natural pursuit. When used as a chase mechanic (Pathfinder Ultimate Campaign rules), the Hellcycle automatically wins any opposed check vs. non-supernatural pursuits — it simply does not have a maximum speed in that context.
>
> *The 120 ft./round figure is a gameplay abstraction, not a physical ceiling. GM note: if a player asks "could the Hellcycle outrun X?", the answer is almost certainly yes unless X has divine or cosmic-level speed.*

## Special Properties

> [!note] Hellfire Wheels (Su)
> The wheels burn with empyreal flame at all times when Ghost Rider is transformed. Any creature making physical contact with the wheels (including being ridden over) takes **4d6 empyreal damage** per contact. This empyreal damage bypasses all fire resistance and immunity — it is the same descriptor as Ghost Rider's hellfire.
>
> The hellfire wheel effect activates [Ghost Rider (Johnny Blaze)](Ghost%20Rider%20%28Johnny%20Blaze%29.md)'s Trail of Hellfire at speed 60+ ft. (see that note for full trail rules: 10 ft. wide × 15 ft. tall, 6d6 empyreal, DC 25 Ref half, persists 1 minute).

> [!note] All-Terrain Movement (Su)
> The Hellcycle traverses any physical surface at full speed with no Ride check and no penalty.
>
> | Surface | Speed |
> |---------|-------|
> | Normal road/ground | 120 ft. |
> | Difficult terrain | 120 ft. (no penalty) |
> | Vertical surfaces (walls, buildings) | 120 ft. |
> | Water surface | 120 ft. |
> | Ice, sand, mud | 120 ft. |
> | Aerial jumps | Parabolic arc at GM's discretion — no hard ceiling, no hard distance cap. A launch from a building rooftop, a highway overpass, or a canyon wall follows logical physics at supernatural scale. The bike does not fly, but it treats gravity as a suggestion during the arc. |
>
> *The previous version stated "30 ft. across, 20 ft. up" as a jump cap — that figure is removed. Ghost Rider has been depicted launching off buildings and arcing across entire city blocks. The arc should be adjudicated by the GM based on launch height, angle, and narrative need.*

> [!note] Remote Recall (Su)
> Ghost Rider can summon the Hellcycle as a **standard action** from anywhere within 10 miles. It arrives in **1d4 rounds**, traveling at its full combat speed along the most direct surface path available (walls, water, any terrain). Physical obstacles that completely seal a space (dimensional barriers, sealed vaults, impossible geometry) may block it — at the GM's discretion.

> [!note] Self-Repair (Su)
> While Ghost Rider is transformed: **5 hp per round**. Damage from Heaven-forged weapons does not regenerate.

> [!note] Bonded Vehicle (Ex/Su)
> Cannot be permanently destroyed while Zarathos is bound to a host. At 0 hp: vanishes in black smoke, **reforms at full hp within 1d10 rounds** within 10 miles of Ghost Rider. When Blaze is in human form, the Hellcycle cannot be permanently destroyed at all — it will always reform.

## Mounted Combat Abilities

> [!warning] Automatic Mounted Feats
> While Ghost Rider is mounted on the Hellcycle, he is treated as having the following feats at no cost — they are properties of the bond, not learned abilities:
>
> | Feat | Effect |
> |------|--------|
> | Mounted Combat | Negate one hit per round with Ride check (DC = attack roll) |
> | Ride-By Attack | Move through threatened squares, attack, continue movement |
> | Spirited Charge | ×2 damage (×3 with reach weapon) on mounted charges |
> | Trample | Overrun targets deal automatic slam damage; no AOO on Ghost Rider |
>
> No Ride check is ever required to control the Hellcycle in any terrain or condition.

> [!note] Rampage (Su)
> **Full-round action · Straight-line path up to 120 ft.**
>
> Ghost Rider performs a charging rampage along a straight line, targeting all creatures in the path.
>
> - **Primary target** (first in path): +28 melee, 4d6+10 bludgeoning + 4d6 empyreal
> - **Secondary targets** (each additional): +22 melee, same damage
> - **DC 24 Fort** or knocked prone (all targets)
> - **DC 24 Ref half** on empyreal component
>
> Ghost Rider may choose to stop anywhere along the path after resolving all attacks. If Trail of Hellfire is active, the rampage path becomes a wall of fire — any creature that was bypassed (not hit) and attempts to pursue must pass through the trail.

## Lore

The Hellcycle was not always supernatural. It was an ordinary motorcycle — Blaze's personal bike — until the night Zarathos first possessed him. The transformation of Johnny's body into the Ghost Rider also transformed his bike, infusing it with empyreal fire and anchoring its existence to the bond between Blaze and Zarathos. It became, in a sense, the third party in their relationship: the physical expression of Ghost Rider's freedom, speed, and reach.

In practical terms, the Hellcycle is Ghost Rider's force multiplier. Without it, he is a fearsome but relatively static combatant — devastating in a corridor, terrifying in a room. On it, he becomes a pursuit engine that cannot be outrun by any natural means, that leaves walls of fire behind it, and that can cross a continent in hours if he chooses. The Trail of Hellfire is one of the most tactically significant abilities in the ensemble: nothing short of flight or teleportation escapes it, and at 15 ft. tall, even low-flying opponents find themselves banking into flame.
