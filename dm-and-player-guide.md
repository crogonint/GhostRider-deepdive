# Ghost Rider — DM & Player Guide

![](images/GR_Haunt.jpg)


*The Canon Reference explains why the build works the way it does. This document explains how to actually run it at the table without burying yourself or your players.*

*Everything here is a recommendation, not a rule. Ghost Rider is a complex enough build that a rigid play style would make him tedious. The goal is to make him feel right.*

---

## The Single Most Important Thing

Ghost Rider is not a combat optimizer. He is a **narrative pressure system**.

His aura is always on. His presence transforms every scene he enters — plants die, water steams, animals flee, and every creature with a soul feels the weight of its own choices. Before a single die is rolled, the DM should be describing what Ghost Rider's arrival does to the environment and the NPCs in it.

If Ghost Rider feels like a fighter with extra steps, the auras aren't being used. If the villain across the room has gone quiet and stopped posturing, you're doing it right.

## Running the Three Forms

![](images/Ghost_Rider2.png)


### Johnny Blaze — Playing the Human

Johnny is the most underrated part of this build. Most tables will want to skip to Ghost Rider immediately. Resist that.

Johnny's scenes establish what Ghost Rider is protecting. If the players never see Johnny being human — buying coffee, arguing with a mechanic, failing to explain his eyes to a confused motel clerk — then Ghost Rider's mercy and restraint don't mean anything because there's nothing to contrast them against.

**At the table:**
- Johnny's Burning Judgment Eyes are always active. He always knows evil is nearby. He always knows what kind. Play this as a constant low-grade weight — he sees the world's sins the way other people see bad weather. He's used to it. It doesn't stop him from being functional. But it never turns off.
- His Hellfire Stigma (the DC 16 dread that bleeds through in human form) should be roleplayed more than rolled. Most NPCs won't know what they're feeling when they stand too close to him. They just want to step back. They don't know why.
- The Hellfire Shotgun is Johnny's way of punching above his weight class without transforming. Use it when he wants to handle something himself — when he'd rather Zarathos not come out to play.
- **Transformation triggers** are worth tracking. The GM should note when those triggers occur and ask the player whether Johnny holds on or lets go. The decision to transform — or not to — is character drama.

### Ghost Rider — The Composite

This is the default form and the most complex to run. The key to not drowning is **the Zarathos Dial**.

#### The Zarathos Dial in Practice

Think of the Dial as Ghost Rider's emotional state made mechanical.

**Blaze Leading (CR 17)** — Johnny is in control. This is Ghost Rider at his most deliberate and most merciful. He uses targeted abilities over area effects. He will offer a chance to flee. The Penance Stare is reserved for those who have genuinely earned it. He suppresses the Hellfire Aura near bystanders without thinking about it. He will not kill someone who surrenders.

*When to use it:* Encounters near civilians. Morally complex situations. Any scene where Ghost Rider needs to be seen as something other than an engine of destruction.

**Composite Default (CR 19)** — The balance. Zarathos wants to burn things. Johnny wants to be precise. The result is controlled ferocity — Ghost Rider is terrifying but purposeful. He uses his full toolkit, doesn't hold back on guilty targets, but isn't burning the city down to get to one demon.

*When to use it:* Standard combat against evil. Most encounters.

**Zarathos Surging (CR 21)** — Zarathos is winning the argument. This happens when the evil is overwhelming, when something genuinely horrifying has occurred, or when Johnny fails the DC 22 Will save at a moment of trauma. In this mode Holy Aura activates automatically, the Hellfire Nova becomes 15d6 with no save, Cold Hellfire is used freely, and the Hellfire Aura cannot be suppressed regardless of who's nearby. Ghost Rider is not thinking about collateral damage right now.

*When to use it:* Sparingly. This mode should feel like an escalation the party is relieved to see — and slightly worried by. It should not become the default just because it has higher numbers.

#### Combat Priority — Composite Form

Ghost Rider has a lot of abilities. Here is a suggested decision tree that keeps him feeling thematic rather than overwhelming.

**Round 1 — Establish presence**
The Existential Dread aura is already doing work. Don't rush. Ghost Rider moves into position. The most guilty target in the room is already looking wrong. Note who fails their save and what tier they're at — track this, it matters later.

If there's a powerful spellcaster: open with the chain. Soul Binding on round 1 removes their mobility and hammers their concentration (−8 penalty). They are now your least urgent problem for several rounds.

If there's a powerful martial threat: Penance Stare. Move action for the opposed Intimidate check, standard action to establish contact. If they meet your gaze voluntarily (some will, out of pride), no check needed.

**Round 2 — Exploit**
Cornugon Smash is free on any Power Attack hit. Against a target already shaken from the aura, a successful demoralize upgrades them to Cowering (Imperious Command). A cowering target is flat-footed, loses Dex to AC, and cannot act. That is the chain: Aura shakes them → hit with Power Attack → free Cornugon Smash → they Cower. Shatter Defenses makes the follow-up attacks flat-footed. Great Cleave chains into nearby targets if things start dropping.

**Penance Stare — Timing**
Don't use the Stare on the first available target. Use it on the right target — the one with the most to answer for. The target with the highest Wis and the worst history takes the longest to drain, but the narrative payoff is greater. A Demon Lord staring into Ghost Rider's eyes for twelve rounds while his army watches is a very different scene than a minion being flatlined in one.

The Stare ends encounters, not fights. Use it when you want the encounter to be over, not when you want to deal more damage.

**Dreadful Carnage — Remember It Exists**
Every time Ghost Rider kills something, all enemies within 30 ft. must save or become shaken. Since they're probably already shaken from the aura, this extends duration. In a prolonged fight against crowds of lesser enemies this ability is doing enormous work that's easy to forget. Track it.

#### What Ghost Rider Does NOT Do

- He does not harm the genuinely innocent. This is mechanical (empyreal fire passes through them) and behavioral. If a villain uses a hostage as a shield, Ghost Rider stops. Johnny reasserts. This is a story beat, not a limitation.
- He does not use the Penance Stare on someone who has done nothing wrong. The stare finds nothing to show them. This has narrative weight — if the Stare fails to lock, the target is probably not guilty of what they're accused of.
- He does not pursue fleeing enemies who are not evil. The guilty run. He follows. The merely frightened run. He lets them go.
- He does not negotiate on behalf of Hell or evil. Zarathos's *wish* SLA — the most powerful ability on the sheet — cannot be used for evil purposes. The divine imperative is still intact under 21 millennia of corruption. This is a hard limit, not a strong suggestion.

### Zarathos Unbound — Running the CR 26 Form

Zarathos unbound is not a player character form in normal campaigns. He is either an antagonist, an emergency, or the climax of a long story arc.

**If he's an antagonist:**
- Soul Hunger is your most important tool. Every session he goes without consuming a soul, he loses 2 to all rolls. A party that understands this can play a siege game rather than a direct fight — contain him, deny him prey, wait for the cumulative penalty to make him beatable.
- The Soulless Bane is his hard counter. If the party can field a genuinely soulless creature or character (construct, specific magical conditions), all his soul-based abilities fail and he takes −4 near them. This is how Mephisto beat him. This is how a prepared party beats him.
- His Aura of Ancient Dread hits Tier 4 evil outsiders particularly hard — panicked plus cowering potential. If the party are fighting alongside demons or devils as uneasy allies, those allies may not be reliable once Zarathos enters the room.

**If he's an emergency:**
- Zarathos unbound is what happens when something appears that Ghost Rider genuinely cannot handle. A demon lord at full strength. A greater deity. An entity that has consumed enough souls to be functionally unkillable by conventional means.
- The party should be aware they are watching something ancient and terrible do what it was designed to do. They should probably not be standing in the way.
- His Soul Consumption means every kill makes him stronger. In a prolonged fight this escalates. Track it. At 5 souls he's applying advanced-template-equivalent bonuses. At 10 he essentially becomes a different creature.

**If he's the climax:**
- The scene is Zarathos asserting full control over Johnny's body at a moment of crisis. Johnny is fighting from the inside. The party is fighting from the outside. Zarathos is fighting everyone.
- The mechanical goal for the party: trigger the Unbreakable Bond. Find the thing that makes Johnny reassert — a loved one's name, the presence of an innocent, a genuine act of mercy that Zarathos cannot process. The DC 22 Will save is Johnny's, not a spell effect. This is a roleplaying challenge, not a combat check.

## The Hellcycle — At the Table

The Hellcycle's **120 ft. tactical speed** is the number that matters in combat. Use it.

Ghost Rider on the Hellcycle is a different tactical problem than Ghost Rider on foot. The Trail of Hellfire (10 ft. wide × 15 ft. tall, 6d6 empyreal) is a battlefield control tool — it cuts zones, forces enemies to path around it or take damage, and persists for a full minute. A Ghost Rider who has made two or three passes through a battlefield has created walls of fire that dictate where everyone can move.

**Rampage** is the Hellcycle's signature attack — a straight-line charge hitting every creature in the path. It pairs naturally with Dreadful Carnage: kill something in the path, trigger shaken on everyone nearby, and the trail seals the retreat route.

**True speed** should be invoked at the table for chase scenes, escapes, and dramatic arrivals. The Hellcycle outpaces any mundane pursuit without a roll. This should feel definitive, not tedious — Ghost Rider either chooses to let something escape, or it doesn't escape. There is no middle ground.

The bike **cannot be permanently destroyed** while Zarathos is bound. If the party destroys it in combat (120 HP, Hardness 20), it reforms within 1d10 rounds nearby. Enemies who focus on the bike are wasting their turns. Ghost Rider knows this. They'll figure it out.

## The Sword of Uri-El — When and Why

The Sword is not a daily carry. It is a solution to a specific problem: an evil that Ghost Rider's standard arsenal cannot permanently end.

**Use it when:**
- The target has regeneration keyed to conditions Ghost Rider cannot meet (good damage, silver, etc.) — the Anathema Strike bypasses regeneration by destroying the creature entirely
- The target respawns in its home plane — a destroyed target does not respawn. There is no soul to return.
- A demon lord needs to be sent a message that survives the encounter — half maximum HP for a year is a meaningful consequence even if they survive
- The party needs Ghost Rider to end something that has been unkillable throughout the campaign

**Don't use it as a default upgrade.** The sword changes the flavor of combat significantly — it shifts Ghost Rider from a judgment engine to something closer to an extinction event. Reserve it for moments that earn that weight.

**The resonance note for Zarathos:** When Zarathos wields it and uses Greater Vital Strike into an Anathema Strike (DC 40 Fort), even a demon lord that makes the save takes 30d6 empyreal and has its maximum HP halved for a year. A second failed save within that year destroys them. This is a two-encounter kill condition on a demon lord. Use it accordingly.

## Existential Dread — Running It Scene by Scene

The Dread aura is always on. Here is how to handle it without rolling dice every six seconds.

**Out of combat:** The DM describes the ambient effect. Animals have left. The plants have gone grey at the edges. NPCs who are guilty of something significant (the DM knows who they are) feel an inexplicable need to confess, leave the room, or avoid eye contact. No rolls unless a specific creature enters the 60 ft. radius and the DM wants to spotlight their reaction.

**At the start of combat:** Roll saves for significant enemies in the first round. For minions, apply the result narratively — some percentage of them are already shaken before Ghost Rider does anything. Don't roll individually for thirty cultists. Roll once for the group and describe it.

**Tier callouts:** When a Tier 4 or 5 entity enters the scene, make it a moment. A demon lord who has never shown fear in centuries of existence suddenly cannot move. An intelligent undead who thought itself beyond judgment realizes it isn't. The DM should pause and describe this. It is a dramatic beat, not a bookkeeping entry.

**The Reaper, legitimate soul-workers:** They sense the Dread but are not subject to terror. If such an entity appears, describe their reaction as recognition rather than fear — a slight nod, perhaps, or a deliberate step back to give Ghost Rider room. Professional courtesy between entities whose work overlaps.

## The Penance Stare — Scene Framing

The Stare is the ability most likely to either be underused (GM forgets to bring it up) or overused (player uses it every single round). Both are wrong.

**Frame it as the end of the scene, not a combat action.** When Ghost Rider locks eyes with a Demon Lord and the lock is established, the combat stops being a combat. It becomes a vigil. Other enemies are either fleeing, attacking uselessly, or frozen in their own dread. The Demon Lord is reliving everything. Ghost Rider is watching without expression. The clock is ticking.

**Give the Demon Lord a voice during the Stare.** They can't act, but they can speak. Some will beg. Some will bargain. Some will curse. Some will, at the end, when Wis is low enough that the defenses are down, say something true. This is the most interesting version of the ability — not the damage, but what comes out of a being when the mask burns away.

**The 1 flat Wisdom on a successful save** means even beings who resist the full weight of the Stare are being slowly worn down. A Greater Deity that makes every save still loses 1 Wis per round. At thirty-one rounds, they're catatonic. They know this. Ghost Rider knows this. The Stare is patient in a way that nothing else in the game is. Play that patience.

## The Guilty, the Innocent, and the In-Between

Ghost Rider's immunity exceptions are the most narratively interesting mechanical feature in the build.

**The genuinely innocent:** Empyreal fire passes through them without effect. The Penance Stare finds nothing. This is a built-in detector. If Ghost Rider uses the Stare on someone and nothing happens — not resistance, not a save, nothing — that person has not willingly harmed another. Whatever they've been accused of, they didn't do it, or at least not that.

**The guilty but remorseful:** There is no mechanical exception for remorse. The fire still burns. The Stare still shows. But Ghost Rider's behavioral code (Blaze's Will) may lead him to allow someone to live through it rather than consuming their soul — to experience the Stare and walk away changed rather than destroyed. This is Ghost Rider at his most merciful, and Johnny's influence is usually responsible for it.

**The in-between:** Most real people. They have sinned in ways great and small. The Stare would show them things, and they would not like it. The damage they take is proportional to what they carry. Ghost Rider does not usually use the Stare on ordinary sinners. That is not what he is for.

## Quick Reference — Round Structure

**Standard Ghost Rider combat round (Composite, chain in hand):**

| Priority | Action | When |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Track Dread saves | Start of round — who's shaken, what tier |
| 2 | Chain: Soul Bind primary spellcaster | Round 1 only, if applicable |
| 3 | Penance Stare: move action (Intimidate), standard action (lock) | If primary target identified |
| 4 | Full attack + Cornugon Smash + Power Attack | Against shaken targets (flat-footed via Shatter Defenses) |
| 5 | Dreadful Carnage trigger | On any kill — automatic, remember it |
| 6 | Trail of Hellfire | Hellcycle at speed, any round — free |

**Things that happen without using actions:**
- Existential Dread aura (always)
- Smoldering Presence (always)
- Burning Judgment Eyes (always)
- Hellfire Aura damage to adjacent enemies (always, unless suppressed)
- Dreadful Carnage (automatic on kill)

**Things that are commonly forgotten:**
- Shatter Defenses — shaken targets are flat-footed when you hit them
- Cornugon Smash — free action on Power Attack hits
- Cold Hellfire Touch (3/day) — the Wis damage option that doesn't require eye contact
- The chain's Soul Bind DC (30 Fort) fires on any successful strike, not just grapple

## A Note on Playing Against Ghost Rider

If Ghost Rider is an NPC antagonist — Zarathos unbound, or a corrupted version pursuing the party for reasons they don't yet understand — the play experience is different.

The party's best tools are:
- **Soulless entities or characters** — Soulless Bane shuts down most of his signature abilities and imposes −4 to attacks and saves within 30 ft.
- **Heaven-forged weapons** — suppress regeneration. Without regen, he can be worn down.
- **Denying soul consumption** — every kill he makes during the encounter makes him stronger. Don't let things die near him if you can help it. Pull your allies back. Let him chase.
- **The loved one's name weakness** — DC 22 Will or he cannot attack that creature for 1 minute. This is Johnny's weakness, not Zarathos's. Against pure Zarathos it may not apply, but it is in the stat block for a reason.
- **Soul Hunger attrition** — if this is a multi-session conflict, keep him from consuming souls. At −10 to all rolls, he becomes beatable by a prepared party.

The party's worst instincts are:
- Trying to out-damage him without suppressing regen first
- Engaging at close range without addressing the Hellfire Aura (4d6/round automatically)
- Using fear effects against him (immune)
- Using mind-affecting effects against Zarathos (immune)
- Splitting the party inside his Aura of Ancient Dread range
